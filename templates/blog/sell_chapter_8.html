Chapter 8: Addressing Concerns and Earning Commitment



Chapter 8 provides a structured approach to handling sales resistance and overcoming buyer objections. It acknowledges that objections are a natural part of the sales process, providing valuable insights into the buyer's concerns. The chapter explores common reasons why prospects raise objections and outlines the five major types of sales resistance: need, product/service, company/source, price, and time. It introduces the LAARC method (Listen, Acknowledge, Assess, Respond, Confirm) as a framework for addressing objections effectively and maintaining a positive dialogue. The chapter also discusses various techniques for earning commitment and closing the sale, emphasizing relationship-building approaches that are more likely to achieve long-term success.

Key Terms and Definitions

  • Sales Resistance: Anything the buyer says or does that slows down or stops the buying process.
  • Need Objection: The buyer expresses that they do not have a need for the product or service.
  • Product or Service Objection: The buyer expresses dissatisfaction with the product or service itself (e.g., features, quality, performance).
  • Company or Source Objection: The buyer expresses concerns about the selling company or the salesperson (e.g., reputation, experience, trustworthiness).
  • Price Objection: The buyer objects to the price, finding it too high or not offering enough value.
  • Time Objection (Stalling Objection): The buyer delays making a decision, often to avoid saying no directly.

LAARC Method

The LAARC method is a structured approach for handling objections:

  • Listen: Pay attention to the buyer's concern.
  • Acknowledge: Show that you understand and respect their viewpoint.
  • Assess: Ask questions to clarify the objection and understand its root cause.
  • Respond: Provide an appropriate and persuasive response.
  • Confirm: Make sure the buyer's concern has been addressed and gain their agreement to move forward.

Techniques for Addressing Objections

  • Forestalling: Addressing a potential objection before the buyer has a chance to raise it.
  • Direct Denial: Telling the buyer directly that their objection is incorrect. Use with caution.
  • Indirect Denial: Softening the blow when correcting an incorrect assumption.
  • Translation or Boomerang: Turning a reason not to buy into a reason to buy.
  • Compensation: Counterbalancing the objection with an offsetting benefit.
  • Questioning or Assessing: Asking questions to better understand the objection and its underlying causes.
  • Third-Party Reinforcement: Using evidence from a credible source to support your response.
  • Feel-Felt-Found: Relating how other customers initially had the same concern but found it to be unfounded.
  • Coming-To-That: Acknowledging the objection and promising to address it later in the presentation.

Commitment Techniques

  • Commitment Signals: Verbal or nonverbal cues that indicate the buyer is ready to make a purchase decision.
  • Direct Commitment: Asking the customer directly for the order.
  • Alternative Choice: Presenting the buyer with a limited number of options to choose from.
  • Summary Commitment: Reviewing all the confirmed benefits and asking for the order based on the value that has been established.
  • T-Account (Balance Sheet) Commitment: A visual technique for listing the pros and cons of the purchase decision.
  • Success Story Commitment: Relating how another customer successfully solved a similar problem using the salesperson's product or service.

Main Concepts

  • Importance of Handling Objections: Objections are a natural part of the sales process and provide an opportunity to understand the buyer's concerns and build trust.
  • Reasons for Objections: Can stem from the need to avoid the sales interaction, lack of information, resistance to change, or genuine concerns about the product, service, company, price, or timing.
  • Types of Sales Resistance: Most objections fall into the categories of need, product/service, company/source, price, or time.
  • LAARC Method for Overcoming Objections: A structured approach that emphasizes listening, understanding, and responding to the buyer's concerns in a respectful and persuasive manner.
  • Earning Commitment and Closing: Salespeople should focus on building value and gaining the buyer's commitment throughout the sales process. Once the buyer is ready to buy, using appropriate closing techniques can secure the sale.

Supporting Evidence

Examples of common buyer objections and effective responses are provided for each objection category. Cases and role plays allow for practicing the LAARC method and different closing techniques. The importance of building trust and addressing concerns is highlighted through real-world examples and ethical dilemmas.
