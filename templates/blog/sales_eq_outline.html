Sales EQ

Chapters

  • Chapter 1: The Mysterious Brown Bag

    This chapter introduces the concept of Sales EQ and its importance in modern selling. It highlights the emotional connection and noncomplementary responses essential for disrupting buyer scripts.

  • Chapter 2: A Perfect Sales Storm

    Blount paints a picture of the challenging sales landscape characterized by informed buyers and disruptive technology, emphasizing the need to adapt and build authentic relationships.

  • Chapter 3: The Irrational Buyer

    This chapter dives into the psychology of buying decisions and how emotions drive choices, introducing concepts like cognitive biases and the significant role they play in sales.

  • Chapter 4: Pattern Painting, Cognitive Biases, and Heuristics

    Blount explains how to create novelty in sales presentations to capture attention and how understanding cognitive biases can increase sales effectiveness.

  • Chapter 5: The Four Levels of Sales Intelligence

    The chapter outlines four types of intelligence critical for sales success and emphasizes continuous learning and emotional intelligence as key differentiators.

  • Chapter 6: Shaping Win Probability

    Blount introduces the idea of "win probability" and strategies that ultra-high performers use to enhance their chances of closing deals.

  • Chapter 7: Dual Process

    This chapter discusses balancing relationship-building with achieving sales objectives, emphasizing the importance of both for successful selling.

  • Chapter 8: Empathy

    The chapter explores the role of empathy in building rapport with stakeholders and understanding their unique emotional drives.

  • Chapter 9: Self-Awareness

    Blount examines the necessity of self-awareness for understanding personal strengths and weaknesses, highlighting tools for developing this skill.

  • Chapter 10: Sales Drive

    The motivational aspect of Sales Drive, combining competitiveness and optimism, is key to maintaining high performance.

  • Chapter 11: Self-Control

    This chapter discusses the significance of self-control in managing emotions and making constructive decisions during high-stress sales situations.

  • Chapter 12: Shaping Win Probability Begins with Qualification

    Emphasizing that rigorous qualification leads to better focus on high-probability prospects, this chapter provides actionable approaches for effective qualification.

  • Chapter 13: Engagement and Micro-Commitments

    Blount illustrates the importance of gaining small, incremental agreements that build trust and advance deals, nurturing a path toward closure.

  • Chapter 14: Stalled Deals and Next Steps

    This chapter addresses the challenge of stalled deals, stressing the necessity of establishing clear next steps to maintain momentum in the sales process.

  • Chapter 15: Sales Process

    Focusing on the structured sales process, Blount discusses its significance in improving consistency and efficiency in achieving sales goals.

  • Chapter 16: Buying Process

    Understanding the buying process and aligning it with the sales process is essential for increasing win probability and providing a smooth customer experience.

  • Chapter 17: The Five Stakeholders You Meet in a Deal

    The chapter introduces the key stakeholder roles in the buying decision and emphasizes the need to understand and strategize around their motivations.

  • Chapter 18: Decision Process

    By addressing emotional drivers and aligning the sales approach with the decision-making process, salespeople can enhance their effectiveness in closing deals.

  • Chapter 19: Do I Like You?

    This chapter discusses the critical role of likability in establishing rapport and trust during the sales process.

  • Chapter 20: Flexing to Complement the Four Primary Stakeholder Personas

    Flexibility in communication style based on stakeholder personas enhances engagement and effectiveness in sales.

  • Chapter 21: Sales Call Agenda Framework

    Providing structure to sales conversations creates clarity, establishes expectations, and enhances stakeholder engagement.

  • Chapter 22: Do You Listen to Me?

    The author emphasizes the importance of active listening and deep listening skills to understand stakeholders better and shape sales outcomes.

  • Chapter 23: Discovery: Sales is a Language of Questions

    Mastery of effective questioning techniques is crucial for uncovering stakeholder needs and fostering rapport.

  • Chapter 24: Do You Make Me Feel Important?

    Making stakeholders feel valued is essential for building trust and influence in sales relationships.

  • Chapter 25: Do You Get Me and My Problems?

    Understanding the stakeholder's unique challenges enables salespeople to craft relevant messages and solutions.

  • Chapter 26: Asking: The Most Important Sales Discipline

    Confidence and assertiveness in asking for commitments are key factors in advancing the sales process.

  • Chapter 27: Turning Around Objections

    Effective objection handling techniques can transform resistance into opportunities for building trust and advancing the sale.

  • Chapter 28: Do I Trust and Believe You?

    Building trust through consistency and integrity is foundational to successful sales relationships.

  • Chapter 29: Amache - The Power of Gratitude

    Empathy and gratitude can significantly enhance personal connections and foster resilience in challenging circumstances.
