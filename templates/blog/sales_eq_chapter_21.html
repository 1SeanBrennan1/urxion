Chapter 21: Sales Call Agenda Framework

This chapter outlines a structured approach to opening sales conversations effectively. The Sales Call Agenda Framework includes four steps: Greeting, Objective, Check, and Frame. Blount explains how using this framework helps salespeople gain control, minimize pitching, and create a more collaborative and productive interaction. He also emphasizes the importance of managing disruptive emotions and using non-complementary behavior to disrupt expectations and pull stakeholders towards you.

Key Terms and Definitions

  • Sales Call Agenda Framework: A structured approach to opening and framing sales conversations to establish control, minimize pitching, and promote stakeholder engagement. The framework includes four steps:
    • Greeting: Setting the tone, demonstrating respect, and confirming the time available for the meeting.
    • Objective: Stating the purpose of the meeting, aligning expectations, and priming the stakeholder for the next step.
    • Check: Asking the stakeholder if there's anything else they want to cover, giving them a sense of ownership and creating an opportunity to address any immediate concerns.
    • Frame: Outlining the conversation structure, emphasizing a collaborative approach, and minimizing the focus on pitching.

Main Concepts

  • The way you open a sales conversation can significantly impact the stakeholder's perception of you and their willingness to engage.
  • The Sales Call Agenda Framework helps you gain control of the conversation, minimize the focus on pitching, and create a more collaborative and productive interaction.
  • Failing to manage disruptive emotions and using a pitching-focused approach can lead to a loss of control, increased resistance, and a decrease in win probability.

Supporting Evidence

  • Handling example of a time-constrained meeting: Illustrating how non-complementary behavior, such as acknowledging the stakeholder's limited time and offering to reschedule, can disrupt their expectations and pull them toward you.
  • The importance of priming: Emphasizing how setting expectations and introducing the next step early on can increase the stakeholder's propensity to say yes later.
  • The three steps of the Sales Conversation Framework: Outlining a structured approach to engaging stakeholders, bridging to solutions, and securing the next step.

Critical Thinking Resources

Critical Thinking Questions

  • How can you use the Sales Call Agenda Framework to create a more positive and productive opening for your sales conversations?
  • What are some strategies for effectively managing your emotions and avoiding pitching when a stakeholder is pressed for time or throws out an early objection?
  • How can you use framing techniques to shift the conversation from a pitching focus to a collaborative problem-solving approach?

Supporting Points

  • Being polite, respectful, and confirming the time available for the meeting sets a professional tone and demonstrates that you value the stakeholder's time.
  • Clearly stating your objective, outlining the conversation structure, and emphasizing a collaborative approach can help you gain control and reduce the stakeholder's anxiety.
  • By asking the stakeholder if there's anything else they want to cover, you give them a sense of ownership and create an opportunity to address their concerns without getting sidetracked by a premature pitch.

Counterpoints

  • Some might argue that following a structured framework can make sales conversations feel rigid and scripted, hindering authenticity and spontaneity.
  • Stakeholders might perceive the Sales Call Agenda Framework as a manipulative tactic to control the conversation.
  • Focusing too much on process might distract salespeople from the importance of building rapport and creating an emotional connection.

Rebuttals

  • The Sales Call Agenda Framework provides a structure for effective communication, not a rigid script. Flexibility and adaptability are still essential for tailoring your approach to individual stakeholders.
  • The framework is designed to enhance the stakeholder's experience by creating a more collaborative and productive interaction, not to manipulate or control them.
  • The framework should be used to support your efforts to build rapport and create an emotional connection, not as a replacement for building relationships.

Discussion Topics

  • The balance between structure and flexibility in sales conversations.
  • The ethical considerations of using framing techniques to influence stakeholder perceptions.
  • The challenges of maintaining control of the conversation while being responsive to stakeholder needs.

Practical Application 1: Implementing the Sales Call Agenda Framework

Actionable Task 21.1: Script Your Opening

  • Steps: Write out a script for your opening, including a greeting, your objective for the meeting, a check for the stakeholder's agenda, and a clear frame for the conversation.
  • Resources: Sales call agenda framework templates, examples from top-performing salespeople in your company.
  • Obstacles/Solutions: Feeling unnatural or scripted? Practice your opening until it feels comfortable and conversational, while still maintaining the key elements of the framework.
  • Timeline: Develop your script within the next week and begin using it in your sales conversations.

Actionable Task 21.2: Master Non-Complementary Behavior

  • Steps: Practice responding to challenging openings (e.g., stakeholder is pressed for time, throws out an early objection) with non-complementary behavior, such as acknowledging their concerns, offering to reschedule, or reframing the situation.
  • Resources: Role-playing with a colleague or sales coach, reviewing examples of non-complementary responses.
  • Obstacles/Solutions: Struggling to remain calm and collected? Take a deep breath, pause before responding, and focus on the desired outcome of the conversation.
  • Timeline: Practice this technique in your next three sales conversations.

Evaluation

Success is measured by a greater sense of control in your sales conversations, a reduction in early objections or resistance, and a smoother transition into the discovery phase.
