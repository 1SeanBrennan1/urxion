Chapter 4: The Importance of Authority and How to Get Past the Gatekeeper

This chapter addresses the challenges of navigating gatekeepers and speaking directly with decision-makers. It highlights:

  • Authority: Projecting authority through your tone of voice, rate of speech, and knowledge about the prospect's industry and challenges.
  • Gatekeeper Script: Developing a script that focuses on controlling the conversation, using questions, interruptions, and techniques for "talking over their head."
  • Gatekeeper Personalities: Understanding the different types of gatekeepers (College Student, Lifer, Office Manager) and adapting your approach accordingly.

Actionable Task 1: Practice Speaking with Authority

Value: Projecting authority over the phone builds credibility and confidence, making you sound more knowledgeable and valuable to the prospect.

How:

  1. Record and analyze your calls: Listen to your call recordings and pay attention to your tone, pace, and word choice. Identify areas where you sound hesitant or unsure.
  2. Practice speaking clearly and confidently: Rehearse your scripts and conversations, focusing on projecting your voice and speaking at a moderate pace.
  3. Eliminate filler words and soft language: Become conscious of using words like "um," "kind of," "maybe," and replace them with more assertive language.

Actionable Task 2: Develop a Gatekeeper Script

Value: A well-structured gatekeeper script increases your chances of getting past gatekeepers and speaking directly to the decision-maker, minimizing time wasted.

How:

  1. Research common gatekeeper objections: Understand the typical questions and responses you might encounter from gatekeepers.
  2. Craft your script with assertive language: Use confident and direct language while remaining polite. Example: "I'm looking for [decision maker], is he available?"
  3. Incorporate techniques like the interrupt: Prepare responses that redirect the conversation and create a sense of urgency. Example: "Is [decision maker] in a meeting? Great, I’ll be brief…".

Actionable Task 3: Analyze Different Gatekeeper Personalities

Value: Understanding the different gatekeeper types allows you to adapt your communication style and overcome their specific objections more effectively.

How:

  1. Identify the gatekeeper type during the call: Pay attention to their tone, language, and how they handle your initial request.
  2. Adjust your approach for each personality: With College Students, focus on authority; with Lifers, build rapport and offer appreciation; with Office Managers, use technical language and go over their head.
  3. Document successful approaches: Note which tactics work best with different gatekeeper personalities to refine your strategy for future calls.
