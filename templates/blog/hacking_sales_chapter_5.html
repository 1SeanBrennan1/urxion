Chapter 5: Lead Research

Going beyond basic contact information, Chapter 5 emphasizes the importance of conducting thorough lead research to personalize outreach and demonstrate value. It introduces the concept of “trigger events” - company news, job changes, industry trends, and other significant events that create opportunities for relevant and timely outreach. Tools like InsideView, Gagein, FunnelFire, DiscoverOrg, and Mention.io are discussed as resources for identifying and monitoring trigger events. The chapter guides readers through gathering key information on prospects from their websites, social media profiles, and news articles, enabling more personalized and effective communication.

Main Concepts

  • Lead research is crucial for crafting relevant messages and providing value in the sales process.
  • Identify "trigger events" (company news, job changes, industry trends) that create opportunities for outreach.

Supporting Evidence

Tools like InsideView, Gagein, FunnelFire, DiscoverOrg, Mention.io, LinkedIn, and Google Alerts are discussed for finding trigger events.

Critical Thinking Questions

  • What types of trigger events are most relevant to your Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) and industry?
  • How can you use trigger events to create personalized and timely outreach messages?

Discussion Topics

  • The "spray and pray" vs. personalized approaches to outbound sales.
  • The importance of balancing research with action in sales.

Implementation Plan

Practical Application 1: Identify Trigger Events

Actionable Task 1.1: Define Trigger Events Relevant to Your ICP

Steps:

  • Company-Level Triggers:
    • Funding Announcements: Have they recently raised a round of funding?
    • New Product Launches: Are they releasing a new product or service?
    • Expansion or Acquisitions: Are they growing rapidly or acquiring other companies?
    • Executive Changes: Have they hired a new CEO, VP of Sales, or other key executives?
    • Awards and Recognition: Have they received recent awards or positive press coverage?
  • Individual-Level Triggers:
    • Job Changes: Have they recently started a new role or been promoted?
    • Content Creation: Are they writing blog posts, articles, or speaking at industry events?
    • Social Media Activity: Are they actively discussing industry trends or challenges?

Resources: Brainstorming, your ICP document, industry publications, news aggregators (e.g., Google News), social listening tools.

Obstacles and Solutions
  • Obstacle: Difficulty finding relevant trigger events - Solution: Set up Google Alerts for keywords related to your ICP, use social listening tools to monitor conversations, follow key industry influencers.

Timeline: Ongoing (make trigger event identification a part of your regular prospecting routine)

Actionable Task 1.2: Use Tools to Automate Trigger Event Monitoring

Steps:

  • Choose a Tool: Explore options like InsideView, Gagein, FunnelFire, DiscoverOrg, Mention.io, or LinkedIn.
  • Set Up Alerts and Tracking: Configure the tool to monitor specific trigger events related to your ICP and to alert you when these events occur.
  • Integrate with CRM: If possible, integrate the trigger event monitoring tool with your CRM to automatically update prospect records.

Resources: Chosen trigger event monitoring tool (free trials or paid plans available), your ICP document, your CRM.

Obstacles and Solutions
  • Obstacle: Cost or integration challenges - Solution: Start with free options or tools that integrate easily with your existing systems. Prioritize the most valuable trigger events for monitoring.

Timeline: 1-2 weeks for tool selection and setup, ongoing monitoring and alert management.

Practical Application 2: Conduct Thorough Lead Research

Actionable Task 2.1: Gather Key Information

Steps:

  • Website Research: Visit the prospect's website to learn about their products, services, target market, and company culture.
  • Social Media Research: Explore their LinkedIn, Twitter, and other relevant social media profiles for insights on their industry involvement, interests, and recent activity.
  • News and Press Coverage: Search for recent news articles or press releases about the company and the individual you're targeting.
  • Use Research Tools: Leverage tools like Discover.ly to quickly gather social media and contact information from within your Gmail inbox.

Resources: Prospect's website and social media profiles, online news aggregators, industry publications, research tools.

Obstacles and Solutions
  • Obstacle: Time-consuming research - Solution: Use tools to streamline the process (like Discover.ly), prioritize research on your most promising leads, or delegate research tasks to a virtual assistant to stay up-to-date on your leads' activities.

Craft Relevant Messages: When a trigger event occurs, send a personalized email congratulating them on their achievement, offering relevant resources, or suggesting a conversation to discuss how you can help.

Examples:
  • Funding Announcement: Send an email congratulating the company on their funding round and offering to help them scale their sales team.
  • New Product Launch: Congratulate the lead on their new product launch and share a case study of how your product helped a similar company achieve success.

Resources: Trigger event monitoring tools, your email marketing or automation platform.

Obstacles and Solutions
  • Obstacle: Lack of time to monitor trigger events - Solution: Prioritize monitoring for high-priority leads or use automation to trigger follow-up messages.

Timeline: Ongoing (monitor trigger events and send follow-ups promptly).

Actionable Task 2.2: Use Holidays and Personal Events as Touchpoints

Steps:

  • Gather Personal Information: Use social media research or tools like Clearbit to find out about your leads' birthdays, work anniversaries, or other relevant personal events.
  • Send Personalized Greetings: Send a brief email or LinkedIn message acknowledging the event and wishing them well.
  • Use Holidays Strategically: Send thoughtful holiday greetings that are relevant to your leads' interests or industries.
Examples:
  • Birthday: Send a simple birthday greeting via email or LinkedIn.
  • Industry Event: Acknowledge a major event in their industry (e.g., "Happy Cybersecurity Awareness Month!") and offer relevant resources.

Resources: Social media, Clearbit, your CRM, email or LinkedIn.

Obstacles and Solutions
  • Obstacle: Finding personal information - Solution: Use social media research, online tools, or simply ask during a conversation. Keep it professional and don't be intrusive.

Timeline: Schedule reminders in advance of holidays or personal events.

Practical Application 3: Master the Art of Following Up

Actionable Task 3.1: Use Tools to Manage Follow-Ups

Steps:

  • Choose a Tool: Explore tools like Boomerang, Rebump, or your email automation platform's follow-up features.
  • Schedule Reminders: Set reminders to follow up with leads at specific intervals or based on trigger events.
  • Use Follow-Up Templates: Create templates for different follow-up scenarios (e.g., after a demo, after a proposal, after a no-response).

Resources: Follow-up tools, your CRM or email automation platform.

Obstacles and Solutions
  • Obstacle: Forgetting to follow up - Solution: Use tools to automate reminders and schedule follow-ups in advance.

Timeline: Ongoing

Actionable Task 3.2: Be Persistent (But Not Annoying)

Steps:

  • Define Your Follow-Up Cadence: Determine how many times you will follow up with a lead before moving them to a long-term nurture list.
  • Space Out Your Touches: Avoid bombarding leads with too many messages in a short period.
  • Provide Value with Each Follow-Up: Offer new insights, resources, or reasons to connect.

Resources: Your sales process documentation, your experience and judgment.

Obstacles and Solutions
  • Obstacle: Balancing persistence with respect for leads' time - Solution: Focus on providing value with each follow-up, and respect a lead's request to stop contacting them.

Timeline: Ongoing

Evaluation Criteria

  • Lead Engagement: Are you seeing increased engagement from your leads (e.g., website visits, email opens, social interactions)?
  • Pipeline Movement: Are nurtured leads converting into opportunities and moving through your sales pipeline?

Additional Resources

Books: "The Sales Magnet" by Kendra Lee, "Jab, Jab, Jab, Right Hook" by Gary Vaynerchuk (for social media marketing).

Online: Nutshell blog (www.nutshell.com/blog/), Close.io blog (blog.close.io).

Lead nurturing is a critical part of a successful sales process. By staying top-of-mind, building relationships, and providing value to your prospects, you'll increase their likelihood of becoming customers!
