Chapter 8: The Manager and the Challenger Selling Model

Chapter 8 highlights the crucial role of frontline sales managers in implementing the Challenger Selling Model. Managers need to be strong coaches, capable of developing Challenger behaviors in their reps. However, coaching is not enough. Managers must also be skilled in "sales innovation" - collaborating with reps to identify and overcome deal-level obstacles and find creative solutions to move deals forward. The chapter emphasizes the need for manager training and tools to support both coaching and sales innovation.

Key Concepts

  • Frontline Manager is Crucial: Managers play a critical role in implementing the Challenger Selling Model.
  • Sales Innovation: Managers must coach reps to identify and overcome deal-level obstacles and find creative solutions.
  • Practical Application: Develop Sales Innovation Skills in Managers.

Explanation: Empower managers to coach for innovation and drive deal momentum.

Actionable Tasks

Train Managers on Identifying Deal Obstacles

  1. Teach managers to ask probing questions to uncover the root cause of stalled deals.
  2. Provide them with frameworks for analyzing customer decision-making processes.

Resources: Sales training professionals, experienced sales leaders, process mapping tools.

Obstacles & Solutions: Managers focusing on superficial issues - encourage them to "peel back the layers" and get to the heart of the problem.

Timeline: As part of manager development programs.

Equip Managers with Brainstorming and Innovation Tools

  1. Introduce managers to techniques like the SCAMMPERR framework.
  2. Provide them with resources and templates to guide brainstorming sessions.

Resources: Innovation experts, brainstorming tools and templates.

Obstacles & Solutions: Lack of manager creativity - create a safe environment for idea generation and encourage experimentation.

Timeline: Ongoing as part of manager development.

Encourage Collaboration and Knowledge Sharing Among Managers

  1. Create a platform for managers to share best practices and successful deal strategies.
  2. Host regular meetings or forums where managers can discuss challenges and brainstorm solutions.

Resources: Online collaboration tools, dedicated meeting time.

Obstacles & Solutions: Managers being reluctant to share their ideas - create a culture that values collaboration and rewards innovative thinking.

Timeline: Ongoing as part of sales management culture.

Evaluation Criteria

  • Increased manager involvement in resolving stalled deals.
  • More creative and effective solutions being implemented.
  • Improved rep performance in handling complex sales situations.

Make Coaching More Effective

What to do (Managers):

  • Stop giving generic advice. Base coaching on specific, observed behaviors from customer calls, using the 6-step pitch as a guide.
  • Coach to the "known." Focus on helping reps execute what's proven to work, not on abstract skills.
  • Get involved in deals. Work with reps to identify obstacles and brainstorm creative solutions.

Key Point: This is a High-Level Start.

Without the book's full context, these actions are a primer. The Challenger Sale provides far more detailed research and frameworks to refine each step. But even these starting points will help you shift your sales approach away from the generic and towards a more Challenger-like mindset.
