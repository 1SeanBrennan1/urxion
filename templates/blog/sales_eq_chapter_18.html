Chapter 18: Decision Process – Understanding the Emotional Journey

Key Concept: The buying decision is an emotional journey, influenced by both logic and feelings.

Blount explores the often-irrational and emotional nature of the decision process. He stresses the importance of aligning the three processes of sales (sales process, buying process, and decision process) and understanding the five key questions stakeholders ask:

  • "Do I like you?"
  • "Do you listen to me?"
  • "Do you make me feel important?"
  • "Do you get me and my problems?"
  • "Do I trust and believe you?"

He also emphasizes the power of social proof in aligning decision-making.

Practical Application 1: Aligning with the Decision Process

Actionable Task 18.1: Identify Emotional Drivers

Steps: During your interactions with stakeholders, listen attentively for clues that reveal their underlying emotional needs, fears, and aspirations related to the decision.

Resources: Active listening skills, emotional intelligence resources, stakeholder research.

Obstacles/Solutions: If you encounter difficulty identifying emotional drivers, ask questions that explore their hopes, fears, and past experiences related to similar decisions.

Timeline: This should be done throughout the sales process, paying close attention to both verbal and nonverbal cues.

Actionable Task 18.2: Address Both Logic and Emotions

Steps: When presenting your solution, acknowledge and address both the logical and emotional aspects of the decision. Provide data and evidence to support your claims, but also connect with the stakeholder's feelings and aspirations.

Resources: Develop compelling stories, testimonials, and analogies that illustrate the emotional benefits of your solution.

Obstacles/Solutions: If a prospect seems overly focused on logic and data, find ways to connect your solution to their personal values, goals, or aspirations to engage their emotions.

Timeline: Incorporate this approach into your presentations, proposals, and all communication with stakeholders.

Evaluation:

Success is measured by the stakeholder's willingness to share their emotional concerns, a shift in the conversation from purely logical arguments to a more personal and relatable level, and a greater sense of trust and connection.
