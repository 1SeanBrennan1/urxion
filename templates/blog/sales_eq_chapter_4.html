Chapter 4: Pattern Painting - Standing Out from the Crowd

Key Concept: The human brain is drawn to novelty. Disrupting expectations and presenting information in a unique and engaging way captures attention and increases memorability.

Key Terms and Definitions:

  • Pattern Painting: Disrupting expectations and differentiating yourself by presenting information in a novel and engaging way.
  • Cognitive Bias: Systematic errors in thinking that arise from the way our brains process information.
  • Heuristic: Mental shortcuts our brains use to make quick decisions in complex situations.

Main Concepts:

  • The human brain is a "pattern monster" that seeks to simplify information processing by recognizing patterns.
  • Disrupting patterns and creating novelty grabs attention and activates the amygdala, the emotional center of the brain.
  • Salespeople can leverage this by "pattern painting" - presenting information in a way that stands out from the ordinary.

Supporting Evidence:

  • The example of a loud noise grabbing attention and activating the fight-or-flight response.
  • The concept of cognitive load and how overwhelming information can impair decision-making.
  • The idea of "different is sexy" - novelty and unexpectedness are inherently appealing.

Practical Application 1: Crafting Memorable Presentations

Actionable Task 4.1: Ditch the Corporate Template

  • Steps: Avoid generic PowerPoint presentations. Use visuals, storytelling, and interactive elements to create a more engaging and memorable experience.
  • Resources: Presentation design tools (Canva, Prezi, Keynote), storytelling workshops, visual communication resources.
  • Obstacles/Solutions: Lack of design skills? Start with simple improvements like using high-quality images, reducing text, and incorporating data visualizations.
  • Timeline: Implement for your next important presentation.

Actionable Task 4.2: Incorporate a Surprise Element

  • Steps: Introduce an unexpected element, like a relevant prop, a personal anecdote, or a thought-provoking question, to disrupt the expected pattern and re-engage the audience.
  • Resources: Brainstorm creative ways to connect your message to the audience's interests or experiences.
  • Obstacles/Solutions: Worried about appearing unprofessional? Choose a surprise element that is relevant to the topic and adds value to the presentation.
  • Timeline: Plan and practice before your next presentation.

Evaluation:

Success is measured by an increase in audience engagement, positive feedback on your presentation style, and improved recall of your key messages.

Critical Thinking Resources:

Critical Thinking Questions:

  • How can you apply "pattern painting" to your sales presentations and conversations?
  • What are some specific strategies for creating novelty and disrupting expectations?
  • How can you leverage cognitive biases and heuristics to influence buyer behavior ethically?

Supporting Points:

  • By understanding the principles of pattern recognition and cognitive biases, salespeople can create more engaging and persuasive messages.
  • Using storytelling, visuals, and humor can make information more memorable and activate emotional responses.
  • Tailoring your message to the individual stakeholder's needs and preferences can create a sense of relevance and importance.

Counterpoints and Rebuttals:

  • Counterpoint: Some might argue that "pattern painting" is manipulative and focuses on style over substance.
  • Rebuttal: "Pattern painting" is about effective communication, not manipulation. It's about making your message clear, concise, and memorable.
  • Counterpoint: Overuse of novelty and disruption can become tiresome and lose its impact.
  • Rebuttal: Novelty and disruption should be used strategically and purposefully, not as a gimmick.
  • Counterpoint: Focusing on cognitive biases can lead to unethical manipulation and exploitation of buyer vulnerabilities.
  • Rebuttal: Understanding cognitive biases is not inherently unethical. It's about understanding human behavior and using that knowledge responsibly.

Discussion Topics:

  • The role of creativity and innovation in modern sales.
  • The ethics of using psychological principles to influence buying decisions.
  • The balance between substance and style in sales messaging.
