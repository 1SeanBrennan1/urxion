Chapter 14: Stalled Deals and Next Steps

Blount addresses the common problem of stalled deals and highlights the "Cardinal Rule of Sales Conversations": Never leave a meeting without a firm next step. He explains how to set clear next steps, overcome the fear of asking, and use calendar invites to solidify commitments. The chapter underscores the importance of maintaining momentum and avoiding the "Call Me Maybe" syndrome.

Key Terms and Definitions

  • Stalled Deals: Opportunities that have lost momentum and are no longer progressing through the sales pipeline.
  • Next Steps: Clear, specific, and mutually agreed-upon actions that move a deal forward toward the next stage of the sales process.
  • The Cardinal Rule of Sales Conversations: Never leave a meeting with a stakeholder without setting and committing to a firm next step.

Main Concepts

Stalled deals are a common problem in sales, often caused by a lack of clear next steps and a failure to maintain momentum. Consistently securing micro-commitments and establishing firm next steps is crucial for keeping deals progressing toward a close. Average salespeople often struggle to get firm next steps due to fear of rejection, lack of assertiveness, or a misunderstanding of the buyer's sense of urgency.

Supporting Evidence

  • The dialogue from a pipeline review illustrates how a lack of clear next steps can lead to stalled deals and a congested pipeline.
  • The example of Ken, the salesperson who failed to schedule a next appointment, demonstrates the consequences of not securing a firm next step while the opportunity is present.
  • The Universal Law of Need emphasizes that desperation from an empty pipeline can lead to poor decision-making and lower win probability.

Practical Application 1: Implementing the Cardinal Rule

Actionable Task 14.1: Never Leave a Meeting Without a Next Step

Steps: Before ending any conversation with a stakeholder, ensure you have a clear understanding of the next action to be taken, who is responsible, and when it will be completed.

Resources: Calendar, task management tool, shared document with stakeholders.

Obstacles/Solutions: If a stakeholder is hesitant to commit, offer alternative next steps or adjust the timeline to find a mutually agreeable option.

Timeline: Implement immediately, making it a non-negotiable part of your sales process.

Actionable Task 14.2: Use Calendar Invites to Solidify Commitments

Steps: After agreeing on a next step, send a calendar invitation to the stakeholder to solidify the commitment and ensure it is scheduled.

Resources: Email calendar, scheduling tool.

Obstacles/Solutions: If the stakeholder declines the invite, follow up promptly to understand their concerns and find a workable alternative.

Timeline: Implement for all next steps, making it a standard practice.

Evaluation

Success is measured by a reduction in the number of stalled deals in your pipeline, an increase in deal velocity, and a more accurate sales forecast.

Critical Thinking Resources

Critical Thinking Questions

  • What are some common reasons why deals stall, and how can you prevent them from happening?
  • What are the key characteristics of a firm next step, and how can you ensure that you get a commitment from the stakeholder?
  • How can you overcome the fear of rejection and become more assertive in asking for next steps?

Supporting Points

  • Clearly defining the next steps, establishing a timeline, and getting agreement from all stakeholders are essential for maintaining momentum.
  • Framing requests as collaborations and highlighting the benefits of taking action can make it easier for stakeholders to commit to next steps.
  • Practicing your ask, visualizing success, and remembering that "no" is not a personal attack can help you overcome the fear of rejection.

Counterpoints

  • Some might argue that being overly assertive in asking for next steps can be off-putting and damage relationships.
  • Focusing too much on next steps can make the sales process feel rigid and impersonal.
  • Stakeholders might not always be willing or able to commit to specific next steps, especially in complex deals.

Rebuttals

  • Assertiveness is not the same as aggressiveness. It's about being clear, confident, and respectful in communicating your expectations.
  • Next steps should be viewed as guideposts for a collaborative sales process, not as a rigid set of rules.
  • Flexibility and adaptability are crucial for navigating complex deals, but it's still essential to establish a framework for moving forward and maintain a sense of momentum.

Discussion Topics

  • The balance between being assertive and being respectful in asking for next steps.
  • The role of trust and transparency in establishing clear expectations for next steps.
  • The challenges of managing stakeholder expectations and timelines in complex sales environments.

Argument Builder

Supporting Points

  • Consistently securing firm next steps creates a sense of progress, accountability, and momentum that drives deals toward a close.
  • By clearly defining expectations for next steps, salespeople can minimize misunderstandings, reduce the risk of stalls, and enhance the overall efficiency of the sales process.
  • Mastering the art of asking for next steps demonstrates professionalism, confidence, and a commitment to delivering value, ultimately increasing win probability.

Counterpoints

  • Some might argue that focusing too much on next steps can create unnecessary pressure and urgency, potentially alienating stakeholders.
  • Stakeholders might perceive a rigid adherence to next steps as a lack of flexibility and a disregard for their individual needs.
  • The constant need to secure next steps can become a distraction from focusing on building relationships and understanding the bigger picture.

Rebuttals

  • Next steps should be viewed as milestones in a collaborative journey, not as a means to pressure or rush stakeholders.
  • Flexibility and adaptability are crucial for navigating complex sales situations, but a clear framework for progress is still essential to maintain momentum and avoid stalls.
  • Next steps should be integrated seamlessly into the overall sales process, focusing on building relationships, understanding needs, and delivering value while driving progress toward a close.
