Chapter 3: List Building – Part 2: Targeting Your High-Potential Buyer

Main Concepts

This chapter focuses on refining your list-building process to target high-potential buyers, often referred to as "whales." It encourages leveraging company databases to gather information such as funding, employee count, job titles, and PR announcements at scale. This data is essential for effectively segmenting your lists and prioritizing outreach efforts.

Supporting Evidence

Tools like CrunchBase, AngelList, Mattermark, Spiderbook, and LinkedIn Sales Navigator are presented as valuable resources for identifying and prioritizing high-value prospects.

Critical Thinking Questions

  • How can you use company data to identify "whales" (high-value prospects) versus smaller fish?
  • What are the key criteria for segmenting your lists based on your specific Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)?

Discussion Topics

  • Data privacy and ethical implications of using company databases for sales prospecting.
  • The evolving role of LinkedIn as a CRM-like tool for salespeople.

Key Concept

Go beyond basic list-building to focus on high-potential buyers or "whales." This means leveraging company databases and targeting tools to find the prospects most likely to generate significant revenue.

Implementation Plan

Practical Application 1: Experiment with Company Databases

Actionable Task 1.1: Select a Database Based on Your Needs

Steps:

  1. Free Options:
    • CrunchBase: Great for startups and tech companies. Offers basic information on funding, team members, and company news.
    • AngelList: Similar to CrunchBase, with a focus on early-stage companies and investors.
  2. Paid Options:
    • Mattermark: Provides Growth Score and Mindshare Score to help you identify high-growth companies.
    • Spiderbook: Goes beyond lead generation to help identify potential partners, references, and competitive insights.
    • LinkedIn Sales Navigator: Powerful filtering options based on job titles, seniority, company size, and more.
  3. Consider Other Options:
    • Glassdoor: Useful for gathering insights on company culture, hiring trends, and salary information.
  4. Choose a database that aligns with your ICP and budget.
  5. Sign up for a free trial or demo.

Resources: Chapter 3, online reviews of company databases, free trial offers.

Obstacles and Solutions:

  • Obstacle: Cost of paid databases – Solution: Start with free options, utilize free trials strategically, negotiate a trial extension, or explore alternative tools that might be more budget-friendly.

Timeline: 1 week for research and trial selection.

Actionable Task 1.2: Build Highly Targeted Lists

Steps:

  1. Define your target criteria by combining your ICP characteristics with database filtering options.
  2. Use multiple filters to enhance precision in your results.
  3. Example: Target companies that have raised Series B funding, have an employee count of over 100, and operate in the software industry.
  4. Leverage Boolean search operators (AND, OR, NOT) to refine your searches.
  5. Create saved searches to easily update your lists as new data becomes available.

Resources: Your chosen database, your ICP document, Boolean search operator guides (available online).

Obstacles and Solutions:

  • Obstacle: Limited filtering options in your chosen database – Solution: Combine data from multiple sources, use creative search terms, or consider a more robust database that better meets your needs.

Timeline: 1-2 weeks for initial setup, ongoing list maintenance and updates.

Practical Application 2: Get Familiar with LinkedIn Sales Navigator

Actionable Task 2.1: Sign up for a Trial and Connect Your Account

Steps:

  1. Visit www.linkedin.com/sales/ and choose a plan.
  2. Connect your LinkedIn account to Sales Navigator for seamless integration.

Resources: Credit card, LinkedIn account.

Obstacles and Solutions:

  • Obstacle: Cost – Solution: Request a discounted trial, explore alternative LinkedIn tools (e.g., LinkedIn Premium), or evaluate if the value justifies the investment.

Timeline: Immediate.

Actionable Task 2.2: Master the Art of Advanced Search

Steps:

  1. Lead Search: Filter by keywords (job titles, skills), company size, industry, location, seniority level, and more.
  2. Account Search: Identify target accounts and use the "TeamLink" feature to find connections within those accounts.
  3. Save your searches and set up alerts to be notified when new leads or accounts match your criteria.
  4. Organize your targeted leads into lists for efficient outreach.

Resources: LinkedIn Sales Navigator, your ICP document, LinkedIn Sales Navigator training resources (available online and through LinkedIn Learning).

Obstacles and Solutions:

  • Obstacle: Learning curve – Solution: Utilize LinkedIn's training materials, watch tutorial videos, practice using different search filters, and seek help from LinkedIn's support team or online communities.

Timeline: Ongoing (make LinkedIn Sales Navigator a regular part of your prospecting workflow).

Evaluation Criteria

  • Database Selection: Are you using the most effective database for your specific needs? Is the data accurate and relevant?
  • List Building: Are you able to create highly targeted lists of "whale" prospects? Are your lists well-organized and updated regularly?
  • LinkedIn Sales Navigator: Are you proficient in using Sales Navigator's advanced search features to find and engage with key decision-makers?

Additional Resources

  • LinkedIn Learning: LinkedIn Sales Navigator courses.
  • Webinars: Many database providers offer free webinars on using their platforms effectively.

Remember, focusing on high-potential buyers can dramatically increase your sales effectiveness. Keep refining your targeting skills, and you'll be well on your way to landing those "whale" deals!
