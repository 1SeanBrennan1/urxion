Chapter 13: Bonus Sales Hacks

This final chapter provides additional "hacks" to enhance your sales efforts. It suggests unconventional but effective ways to make a memorable impact and build stronger connections with prospects. The chapter encourages using your email signature and out-of-office reply strategically as marketing opportunities. It emphasizes the power of injecting humor and personality into your communication (appropriately and authentically). It also explores creative offline touchpoints, like sending personalized letters or thoughtful gifts, to stand out from the digital noise and foster deeper relationships.

Main Concepts

  • Leverage your email signature and out-of-office reply for marketing and lead generation.
  • Inject humor into your communication to stand out and build rapport.
  • Utilize unexpected tactics like offline mail or gifts to create memorable experiences.

Supporting Evidence

  • Examples are provided of using links to positive press in email signatures and OOO replies.
  • MailLift is highlighted as a service for sending handwritten letters at scale.

Critical Thinking Questions

  • What are some creative ways to use humor in your sales process?
  • What are the ethical considerations of using "frenemy" tactics on LinkedIn?

Discussion Topics

  • The role of creativity and experimentation in sales.
  • The boundaries of ethical sales practices.

Implementation Plan

Practical Application 1: Leverage Your Email Signature and OOO Reply

Actionable Task 1.1: Craft a Compelling Email Signature

Steps:

  1. Keep It Simple: Avoid clutter and unnecessary images. Use a professional font and a clean layout.
  2. Highlight a Recent Success: Include a link to a recent positive press article, blog post, case study, or customer testimonial.
  3. Use a Call to Action: Consider adding a brief call to action (e.g., "Schedule a demo," "Download our free ebook").

Example:

[Your Name] | [Your Title]
[Your Company]
[Your Phone Number] | [Your Email Address]
[Compelling Headline] - [Link to Content]

Resources: Your recent company news or blog posts, case studies, customer testimonials.

Obstacles and Solutions:

  • Obstacle: Lack of recent positive press - Solution: Feature a blog post, case study, or customer testimonial instead. You can also create a landing page with a free resource or offer.

Timeline: 1-2 hours

Actionable Task 1.2: Turn Your OOO Reply into a Marketing Opportunity

Steps:

  1. Go Beyond the Generic: Instead of a standard "I'm out of the office" message, use your OOO reply to promote your company or a recent success.
  2. Include a Link to Valuable Content: Share a link to a blog post, case study, or recent press article.
  3. Provide an Alternative Contact: Make sure to include contact information for someone who can assist while you're unavailable.

Example:

I'm currently out of the office, returning on [Date].
In the meantime, check out our latest blog post: [Link to Content]
For urgent matters, please contact [Colleague's Name] at [Colleague's Email Address].

Resources: Your company's blog or website, recent press releases or articles, contact information for a colleague.

Obstacles and Solutions:

  • Obstacle: Company restrictions on OOO replies - Solution: Work within your company's guidelines, or consider setting up an autoresponder for your personal email account that forwards leads to your company's inbox.

Timeline: 15-30 minutes

Practical Application 2: Inject Humor and Personality into Your Communication

Actionable Task 2.1: Use Humor Appropriately (and Sparingly)

Steps:

  1. Know Your Audience: Humor is subjective, so make sure it's appropriate for the lead's industry and personality.
  2. Keep It Lighthearted: Avoid offensive or controversial humor.
  3. Don't Overdo It: Use humor sparingly to add a touch of personality, not to dominate the conversation.

Examples:

  • Share a relevant and amusing anecdote in an email.
  • Use a lighthearted GIF or meme (if appropriate for the context).
  • Inject a bit of self-deprecating humor to show you don't take yourself too seriously.

Resources: Your sense of humor, online resources for funny GIFs or memes.

Obstacles and Solutions:

  • Obstacle: Uncertainty about what's appropriate - Solution: When in doubt, err on the side of caution. Observe how your leads use humor in their communication and adjust your approach accordingly.

Timeline: Ongoing (experiment with humor and see what works)

Actionable Task 2.2: Showcase Your Personality (Authentically)

Steps:

  1. Be Yourself: Don't try to be someone you're not. Authenticity builds trust.
  2. Find Common Ground: Look for shared interests or experiences to connect with your leads on a personal level.
  3. Use Your Own Voice: Let your personality shine through in your writing and speaking style.

Resources: Your own experiences, interests, and communication style.

Obstacles and Solutions:

  • Obstacle: Feeling pressure to be overly formal or professional - Solution: Relax and be yourself. Remember that people buy from people they like and trust.

Timeline: Ongoing

Practical Application 3: Get Creative with Offline Touchpoints

Actionable Task 3.1: Send Personalized Mail (Letters or Gifts)

Steps:

  1. Choose Your Method:
    • Handwritten Letters: Use a service like MailLift to send personalized, handwritten letters at scale.
    • Gifts: Send thoughtful gifts, such as books, gift baskets, or company swag.
  2. Make it Relevant: Choose gifts or content that align with the lead's interests or industry.
  3. Don't Expect an Immediate Return: Think of this as a long-term investment in relationship building.

Examples:

  • Handwritten Letter: Thank a lead for their time after a meeting, or send a personalized note congratulating them on a recent achievement.
  • Gift: Send a book related to their industry or a gift basket with local treats.

Resources: MailLift (or similar services), online gift retailers.

Obstacles and Solutions:

  • Obstacle: Finding the lead's mailing address - Solution: Use online tools (e.g., Clearbit, ZoomInfo) to find company addresses, or simply ask the lead during a conversation (e.g., "Would it be alright if I sent you some additional information?").

Timeline: As appropriate (after a meeting, during a holiday season, after a deal is closed)

Evaluation Criteria

  • Engagement and Response: Are you seeing increased engagement from your leads (e.g., email replies, social interactions, meeting requests)?
  • Deal Progression: Are your unconventional tactics helping to move deals forward and build stronger relationships?

Additional Resources

  • Books: "Giftology" by John Ruhlin (the art of gift-giving in business), "The Thank You Economy" by Gary Vaynerchuk (the importance of gratitude and appreciation in business).
  • Online: Sales Hacker blog (www.saleshacker.com).

Remember, in a world of digital noise, sometimes the most effective way to stand out is to go offline and make a personal connection. Be creative, be authentic, and don't be afraid to experiment!
