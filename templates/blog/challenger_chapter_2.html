Chapter 2: The Challenger (Part 1): A New Model for High Performance

This chapter introduces the five distinct sales rep profiles: Hard Worker, Challenger, Relationship Builder, Lone Wolf, and Reactive Problem Solver. Research reveals that Challengers consistently outperform other profiles, especially in complex sales environments. Their success stems from their ability to teach customers valuable insights, tailor their message to different stakeholders, and take control of the sales conversation. The key takeaway is that Challengers are not simply high performers in tough times; they are the new model for high performance in complex sales, regardless of economic conditions.

Key Concepts

  • Five Sales Rep Profiles: Hard Worker, Challenger, Relationship Builder, Lone Wolf, Reactive Problem Solver.
  • Challenger Advantage: Challengers outperform other profiles, especially in complex sales environments.
  • Practical Application: Develop a Challenger-Focused Hiring and Development Plan.

Explanation: Target the right talent and develop Challenger skills across your team.

Actionable Tasks

Identify Current Rep Profiles

Steps:

  1. Use a sales rep profile assessment tool (like the one in Appendix B or a similar commercially available tool).
  2. Administer the assessment to your sales team.

Resources: Assessment tool, HR team, sales managers.

Obstacles & Solutions: Low rep engagement with assessment - emphasize the value for individual development and make it part of performance reviews.

Timeline: 2 weeks.

Create a Challenger Hiring Profile

Steps:

  1. Define the specific skills, behaviors, and knowledge that define a Challenger in your industry.
  2. Develop interview questions and assessment exercises to evaluate Challenger competencies (see Appendix C).

Resources: HR team, top-performing Challengers, sales leadership.

Obstacles & Solutions: Difficulty attracting Challenger talent - emphasize career growth opportunities and a culture that values innovation and customer impact.

Timeline: 3 weeks.

Develop a Challenger Development Program

Steps:

  1. Design a training program that focuses on teaching, tailoring, and taking control, while leveraging constructive tension.
  2. Incorporate role-playing, case studies, and real-world application exercises.

Resources: Training professionals, top-performing Challengers, sales enablement tools.

Obstacles & Solutions: Lack of internal training expertise - partner with external training providers or leverage online resources.

Timeline: 8 weeks.

Evaluation Criteria

  • Increased percentage of Challengers hired for new roles.
  • Improved performance of core performers who receive Challenger training (track sales metrics, customer satisfaction, etc.).
