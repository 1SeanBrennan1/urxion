Chapter 11: Preparing for and Holding Your First Sales Call

This chapter focuses on making the most of your first sales call - a critical moment for building rapport, understanding the lead's needs, and setting the stage for a successful sales journey. It emphasizes thorough preparation, including reviewing lead research, identifying key pain points, and tailoring your approach based on the lead's specific context. It recommends using call preparation tools like Refresh.io and Charlie App to gather insights and talking points before the call. The chapter provides guidance on conducting an effective discovery call, using targeted questions to uncover the lead's past experiences, current situation, desired outcomes, and budget considerations. It also emphasizes the importance of a strong opening, building rapport, and transitioning smoothly to next steps.

Main Concepts

  • Preparation is key for successful sales calls.
  • Use discovery questions to understand the prospect's needs, past experiences, and buying process.
  • Tools like Refresh.io and Charlie App can provide helpful prospect dossiers.

Supporting Points

  • A list of key discovery questions is provided.
  • The chapter emphasizes the importance of active listening, diagnosis, and prescribing solutions.

Counterpoints

In some cases, jumping straight into a demo might be more effective than a lengthy discovery call.

Rebuttals

While a demo-first approach can work for some products or industries, understanding the prospect's needs and context through discovery is often essential for tailoring the demo and closing the deal.

Critical Thinking Questions

  • How can you adapt your discovery questions to different types of prospects and situations?
  • What are some effective ways to transition from discovery to a presentation or demo?

Discussion Topics

  • The balance between asking questions and presenting information during a sales call.
  • The role of empathy and building rapport in successful sales conversations.

Implementation Plan

Practical Application 1: Gather Information and Prepare for Your Call

Actionable Task 1.1: Review Lead Research and Trigger Events

  1. Revisit Your CRM or Research Notes: Review all the information you've collected on the lead - their company, job title, trigger events, social media activity, etc. (see Chapters 3 and 5).
  2. Identify Key Pain Points: Based on your research, identify the lead's likely challenges or areas where your product or service could provide value.
  3. Tailor Your Approach: Adjust your introduction and opening questions to address the lead's specific situation and interests.

Resources: Your CRM, research notes, trigger event monitoring tools.

Obstacles and Solutions:

Obstacle: Limited information on the lead - Solution: Conduct additional research before the call or use your opening questions to gather more information.

Timeline: 15-30 minutes before the call.

Actionable Task 1.2: Use Call Preparation Tools (Dossiers)

  1. Choose a Tool: Explore options like Refresh.io, Charlie App, or your CRM's call preparation features.
  2. Connect to Your Calendar: Link the tool to your calendar so it can automatically pull information on the people you're meeting with.
  3. Review the Dossier: Take a few minutes before your call to review the dossier provided by the tool. This will give you talking points and help you personalize your approach.

Resources: Call preparation tools (free trials or paid plans available), your calendar.

Obstacles and Solutions:

Obstacle: Difficulty finding relevant information - Solution: The quality of information provided by these tools can vary. Be prepared to supplement the dossier with your own research.

Timeline: 5-10 minutes before the call.

Practical Application 2: Conduct an Effective Discovery Call

Actionable Task 2.1: Start Strong and Build Rapport

  1. Make a Positive Impression: Be on time, be enthusiastic, and use the lead's name.
  2. Set the Agenda: Briefly explain the purpose of the call and what you hope to accomplish.
  3. Ask Open-Ended Questions: Start with open-ended questions to get the lead talking about their challenges and needs.

Examples:

  • "I understand you're the VP of Marketing at [Company]. Can you tell me a bit about your current marketing priorities?"
  • "What are some of the biggest challenges you're facing in [relevant area]?"

Resources: Your prepared notes, active listening skills.

Obstacles and Solutions:

Obstacle: The lead is hesitant to open up - Solution: Try building rapport by finding common ground, referencing a trigger event, or sharing a brief, relevant anecdote.

Timeline: First 5 minutes of the call.

Actionable Task 2.2: Ask Targeted Discovery Questions

The Past: Uncover their past experiences and buying process.

  • "When was the last time you purchased a solution for [relevant area]?"
  • "What was that experience like? What worked well, and what could have been better?"

The Present: Understand their current situation and pain points.

  • "What are you currently using to address [challenge]?"
  • "What are the biggest pain points you're experiencing with your current approach?"

The Future: Explore their goals and desired outcomes.

  • "What are you hoping to achieve by implementing a new solution?"
  • "What would your ideal outcome look like?"

The Budget: Gently address their budget and timeline.

  • "Do you have a budget allocated for a solution in this area?"
  • "What is your timeline for making a decision?"

Resources: Your prepared notes, a list of targeted discovery questions.

Obstacles and Solutions:

Obstacle: The lead is unwilling to share budget information - Solution: Focus on building trust and demonstrating the value of your solution before asking about budget.

Timeline: The bulk of the discovery call (15-20 minutes).

Actionable Task 2.3: Transition to Next Steps and Schedule a Follow-Up

  1. Summarize Key Findings: Recap the lead's key pain points and desired outcomes.
  2. Position Your Solution: Briefly explain how your product or service can address their specific needs.
  3. Propose a Next Step: Suggest a clear next step, such as a demo, a more in-depth discussion, or sharing a relevant resource.
  4. Schedule a Follow-Up: If the lead is interested, schedule a follow-up meeting or call while you're on the phone.

Resources: Your calendar, email, or CRM.

Obstacles and Solutions:

Obstacle: The lead is not ready to commit to a next step - Solution: Offer to send them additional information or resources to help them evaluate their options. Nurture the lead and follow up at a later time.

Timeline: Last 5-10 minutes of the call.

Practical Application 3: Develop a Call Script (Optional)

Actionable Task 3.1: Create a Basic Script Outline

  1. Introduction: Start with a strong opening that grabs the lead's attention and builds rapport.
  2. Discovery Questions: Include a list of targeted questions to uncover their needs and challenges.
  3. Solution Overview: Prepare a concise overview of your product or service and its key benefits.
  4. Call to Action: Have a clear call to action and be prepared to handle objections.

Resources: Brainstorming, examples of call scripts, your sales process documentation.

Obstacles and Solutions:

Obstacle: The script feels too rigid or unnatural - Solution: Use the script as a guide, but be flexible and adapt to the conversation.

Timeline: 1-2 days (revise and refine your script over time).

Evaluation Criteria

  • Call Quality: Are your discovery calls effective at uncovering leads' needs and building rapport?
  • Conversion Rate: Are you converting a high percentage of discovery calls into opportunities or next steps?

Additional Resources

  • Books: "SPIN Selling" by Neil Rackham, "The Sales Magnet" by Kendra Lee.
  • Online: Gong.io blog (www.gong.io/blog/), Sales Hacker blog (www.saleshacker.com).

The first sales call is your chance to make a lasting impression. By preparing thoroughly, asking the right questions, and demonstrating value, you can set the stage for a successful sales process.
