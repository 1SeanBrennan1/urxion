Chapter 7: Taking Control of the Sale

Taking control is the focus of Chapter 7. Challengers are skilled at asserting control throughout the sales process, not just during price negotiations. They are comfortable discussing money and confident in the value they provide. This chapter emphasizes the difference between assertiveness and aggression, offering practical techniques for managing tension, handling concessions, and driving decision-making. Key skills include pushing for early access to key stakeholders, practicing "productive pushback" to customer demands, and developing a structured approach to negotiation.

Key Concepts

  • Proactive Control: Challengers take control throughout the sales process, not just during price negotiations.
  • Comfort with Tension: Challengers are willing to challenge customer thinking and push for decision-making.
  • Negotiation Skills: Challengers are skilled at managing tension, handling concessions, and driving towards a mutually beneficial outcome.

Practical Application: Develop Negotiation and Assertiveness Skills

Equip reps to be more assertive and take control of the sales conversation.

Actionable Tasks

1. Implement a Negotiation Training Program

Steps:

  • Utilize a structured negotiation framework (like the DuPont method or the SSN approach).
  • Incorporate role-playing exercises, case studies, and real-world scenarios.

Resources: Negotiation training professionals, sales managers, negotiation tools, and templates.

Obstacles & Solutions: Reps may view negotiation as a "hard sell" tactic; it’s essential to emphasize the value of collaboration and finding mutually beneficial solutions.

Timeline: 4-6 weeks.

2. Coach Reps on Assertiveness Techniques

Steps:

  • Help reps understand the difference between assertiveness and aggression.
  • Provide them with specific language and approaches for pushing back on customer demands and driving decision-making.

Resources: Sales managers, role-playing exercises, and feedback sessions.

Obstacles & Solutions: Reps may be reluctant to challenge customers; it’s important to reinforce the value of the rep's expertise and the need to create a sense of urgency.

Timeline: Ongoing as part of coaching activities.

Evaluation Criteria

  • Improved rep performance in handling price negotiations.
  • Increased customer buy-in to proposed solutions.
  • Reduced sales cycle time due to improved decision-making.
