Chapter 3: The Challenger (Part 2): Exporting the Model to the Core

Chapter 3 focuses on how to build a Challenger sales force. The authors emphasize that Challengers are made, not just born, and their skills can be developed. They introduce the Challenger Selling Model, built on the pillars of teaching, tailoring, and taking control, while leveraging constructive tension. Importantly, implementing this model is not a quick fix but a journey that requires organizational change, not just individual development. Companies need to invest in all elements of the model - training, coaching, tools, and aligning organizational capabilities - to fully realize the benefits.

Key Concepts

  • Challengers Are Made, Not Born: Challenger skills can be taught and developed.
  • Combination of Skills is Key: Teaching, tailoring, and taking control are most effective when combined.
  • Organizational Capability is Crucial: Building a Challenger sales force requires a shift in organizational capabilities, not just individual rep skills.

Practical Application: Build Organizational Support for Challenger Selling

Explanation: Align your entire organization to support the Challenger approach.

Actionable Tasks

1. Develop a Cross-Functional Challenger Team

Steps:

  1. Involve representatives from sales, marketing, sales enablement, and training.
  2. Define clear roles and responsibilities for each team member.

Resources: Executive sponsorship, dedicated team resources, communication channels.

Obstacles & Solutions: Lack of cross-functional alignment - secure executive sponsorship and create a shared vision for the Challenger transformation.

Timeline: 2 weeks.

2. Create a System for Capturing and Sharing Best Practices

Steps:

  1. Develop a process for capturing Challenger-related successes, insights, and customer stories.
  2. Create a platform for sharing best practices (e.g., internal knowledge base, online forum, regular meetings).

Resources: Collaboration platform, content management system.

Obstacles & Solutions: Low rep participation in knowledge sharing - incentivize contributions and recognize top contributors.

Timeline: 4 weeks.

3. Align Compensation and Recognition Systems

Steps:

  1. Review current compensation and recognition programs to ensure they reward Challenger behaviors.
  2. Modify programs as needed to incentivize teaching, tailoring, and taking control.

Resources: HR team, sales leadership, compensation experts.

Obstacles & Solutions: Resistance to changing compensation structures - demonstrate the financial impact of Challenger behaviors on sales performance.

Timeline: 6 weeks.

Evaluation Criteria

  • Increased cross-functional collaboration on Challenger initiatives.
  • Active participation in knowledge sharing platforms.
  • Demonstrated shift in rep behavior towards Challenger principles.
