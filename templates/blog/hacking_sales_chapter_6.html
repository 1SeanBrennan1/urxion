Chapter 6: Segmenting

Chapter 6 emphasizes the power of segmenting your lead lists to personalize your outreach at scale. By grouping leads based on shared characteristics such as industry, company size, trigger events, or technology used, you can tailor your messaging to resonate more deeply with their specific needs and interests.

Main Concepts

  • Segmenting lists based on relevant criteria is crucial for personalized messaging at scale.
  • Segmentation factors can include company size, industry, location, technology used, recent events, and more.

Supporting Evidence

  • The Storefront case study highlights the power of segmenting by neighborhood for a personalized outreach campaign.
  • LinkedIn's InMail credit policy demonstrates how platforms reward targeted outreach.

Implementation Plan

Practical Application 1: Segment Your Lists Based on Key Criteria

Actionable Task 1.1: Choose Segmentation Factors

Steps:

  1. Review Your ICP: Identify the most important characteristics that differentiate your ideal customers.
  2. Consider Data Points: Look at the data you've collected on your leads - company size, industry, location, technology used, job titles, trigger events, etc.
  3. Select Relevant Factors: Choose 2-3 key factors that will have the most impact on how you tailor your messaging.

Examples:

  • Industry: Segment leads by industry (e.g., software, healthcare, manufacturing) to tailor your messages to specific challenges and use cases.
  • Company Size: Segment by company size (e.g., small business, mid-market, enterprise) to adjust your value proposition and pricing options.
  • Trigger Events: Segment by recent trigger events (e.g., funding announcement, new product launch) to make your outreach timely and relevant.
  • Technology Used: Segment based on the technologies your leads are currently using (through tools like Datanyze or BuiltWith) to highlight relevant integrations.

Resources: Your ICP document, lead data from your CRM or databases.

Obstacles and Solutions:
  • Obstacle: Over-segmenting (creating too many small groups) - Solution: Start with broader segments and get more specific as needed. Focus on the factors that will make the biggest difference in your messaging.

Timeline: 1-2 days

Actionable Task 1.2: Create Segmented Lists in Your CRM or Spreadsheet

Steps:

  1. Use Filtering Tools: Most CRMs and spreadsheet programs allow you to filter and sort your data based on specific criteria.
  2. Create New Lists or Tags: Create new lists or apply tags to your leads to organize them into your chosen segments.
  3. Maintain List Hygiene: Keep your segmented lists up-to-date as you collect new information or as leads' situations change.

Resources: Your CRM or spreadsheet software.

Obstacles and Solutions:
  • Obstacle: CRM limitations or lack of segmentation features - Solution: Consider upgrading your CRM or using a spreadsheet program (like Google Sheets) for segmentation if your CRM lacks the necessary features.

Timeline: 1-2 days for initial setup, ongoing maintenance.

Practical Application 2: Craft Tailored Messages for Each Segment

Actionable Task 2.1: Write Personalized Email Templates

Steps:

  1. Focus on Value: Highlight the specific benefits and solutions that are most relevant to each segment's needs and pain points.
  2. Use Trigger Events: If your segment is based on a recent event, use that event as a conversation starter.
  3. Keep It Concise and Actionable: Make your emails easy to read and include a clear call to action (e.g., schedule a call, download a resource).

Example (Segment: SaaS companies with recent funding announcement):

Subject: Congrats on the Funding - [Your Company] Can Help You Scale

Body: Hi [Lead Name],
I saw the news about your recent funding round - congrats! As you enter this exciting growth phase, [your company] can help you... [explain how your product or service helps companies in their situation]. Would you be open to a quick call to discuss how we can help you achieve your growth goals?

Resources: Your ICP document, segmented lead lists, email marketing software (if available), email template guides (available online).

Obstacles and Solutions:
  • Obstacle: Writer's block - Solution: Use email template examples as a starting point, get feedback from colleagues, or hire a freelance copywriter.

Timeline: 1-2 days per email template

Actionable Task 2.2: A/B Test and Optimize Your Messages

Steps:

  1. Create Variations: Test different subject lines, calls to action, and email body copy.
  2. Use Email Marketing Tools: Tools like Mailchimp or Constant Contact allow you to easily A/B test your emails.
  3. Track Key Metrics: Monitor open rates, click-through rates, and response rates to see which versions perform best.
  4. Continuously Iterate: Use the data to make adjustments to your templates and improve your results over time.

Resources: Email marketing software (free or paid plans available), your CRM (if it has email marketing capabilities).

Obstacles and Solutions:
  • Obstacle: Lack of email marketing expertise - Solution: Utilize the resources and support provided by your chosen email marketing platform.

Timeline: Ongoing testing and optimization.

Evaluation Criteria

  • Segmentation Effectiveness: Are your open rates, click-through rates, and response rates higher for segmented email campaigns compared to non-segmented campaigns?
  • Message Relevance: Are your leads finding your messages valuable and relevant to their specific needs?

Additional Resources

  • Books: "Hacking Growth" by Sean Ellis and Morgan Brown (for A/B testing), "The Challenger Sale" by Matthew Dixon and Brent Adamson (for understanding customer needs and crafting tailored messages).

Segmentation is a powerful way to make your sales outreach more effective. By taking the time to understand your leads and tailor your communication, you'll be able to stand out from the noise and build stronger connections.
