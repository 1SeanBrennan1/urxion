Chapter 7: Dual Process

This chapter challenges the myth that sales is solely about building relationships. Blount argues that while relationships are crucial, the ultimate goal is closing deals. He introduces the concept of "Dual Process," which involves balancing the focus on relationships and achieving sales objectives. He highlights the importance of Sales EQ in navigating this dual process and detaching from the need to be liked for the sake of achieving goals.

Key Terms and Definitions

  • Dual Process: The ability to balance the focus on building relationships and achieving sales objectives.

Main Concepts

  • Sales is not solely about building relationships; the ultimate goal is closing deals.
  • While relationships are important, salespeople must avoid getting sidetracked by them.
  • Sales EQ helps balance the investment in interpersonal relationships and the pursuit of sales objectives.

Supporting Evidence

  • The contrast between salespeople who are good at making friends but bad at closing deals and those who are effective at both.
  • The concept of "people-people" and their potential struggles in regulating their need to be liked.
  • The importance of detachment from the egocentric need to be liked to achieve objectives.

Critical Thinking Resources

Critical Thinking Questions

  • How can you find the right balance between building relationships and closing deals?
  • What are some strategies for managing your own need for likeability without sacrificing your sales goals?
  • How can you use Sales EQ to navigate the dual process effectively?

Supporting Points

  • Prioritizing building trust and understanding the customer's needs can lead to stronger relationships and higher closing rates.
  • Focusing on delivering value and solving the customer's problems can shift the focus away from purely transactional aspects.
  • Recognizing and managing your own emotional triggers can help you maintain professionalism and objectivity during challenging conversations.

Counterpoints

  • Some might argue that prioritizing closing deals over building relationships can damage trust and limit long-term success.
  • Focusing solely on objectives can make salespeople appear impersonal and transactional.
  • Detachment from the need to be liked might make salespeople appear cold and uncaring.

Rebuttals

  • Building strong relationships should not come at the expense of achieving sales objectives. It's about finding the right balance.
  • Focusing on objectives does not mean neglecting the human element. It's about understanding the customer's needs and delivering solutions that create value.
  • Detachment from the need to be liked is not about being cold. It's about maintaining professionalism and avoiding getting sidetracked by personal insecurities.

Discussion Topics

  • The challenges of balancing relationships and sales goals in different sales environments.
  • The role of empathy and emotional intelligence in navigating the dual process effectively.
  • The ethical considerations of detaching from the need to be liked while maintaining customer focus.

Argument Builder

Supporting Points

  • Mastering the dual process allows salespeople to achieve both short-term and long-term success by balancing relationship-building and closing deals.
  • Sales EQ provides the tools and skills needed to manage emotions, build rapport, and navigate complex sales situations effectively.
  • By understanding the buyer's perspective and aligning your approach accordingly, you can create win-win solutions that foster trust and loyalty.

Counterpoints

  • Focusing on both relationships and sales objectives can be overwhelming and lead to indecision.
  • The dual process might require salespeople to adapt their approach significantly, which can feel uncomfortable or inauthentic.
  • Balancing empathy with detachment might require a level of emotional intelligence that is difficult to achieve.

Rebuttals

  • With practice and experience, salespeople can develop the skills and intuition needed to navigate the dual process effectively.
  • Authenticity and integrity are not compromised by adapting your communication style. It's about finding common ground and building rapport.
  • Developing emotional intelligence is an ongoing journey, and even small improvements can have a significant impact on sales performance.

Practical Application 1: Shifting from "People-Pleaser" to "Trusted Advisor"

Actionable Task 7.1: Define Your Boundaries

  • Steps: Set clear expectations for yourself and your prospects regarding your time, availability, and willingness to make concessions.
  • Resources: Reflect on past experiences where you felt taken advantage of or where you compromised your values. Develop a personal set of guidelines.
  • Obstacles/Solutions: Feeling guilty about saying "no"? Practice assertiveness techniques and remind yourself that you are providing value and deserve to be respected.
  • Timeline: Implement immediately, starting with small steps to assert your boundaries.

Actionable Task 7.2: Focus on Value, Not Likeability

  • Steps: Shift your mindset from seeking approval to delivering value. Focus on understanding the prospect's needs and presenting solutions that address their challenges.
  • Resources: Develop a value proposition that clearly articulates the benefits you offer to your target market.
  • Obstacles/Solutions: Worried about appearing too assertive? Practice framing your value propositions in a way that is both confident and helpful.
  • Timeline: Revise your current sales materials and practice incorporating value-focused language into your conversations.

Evaluation

Success is measured by an increase in your confidence, a reduction in feelings of resentment or being taken advantage of, and a shift in prospect interactions towards a more balanced and respectful exchange.
