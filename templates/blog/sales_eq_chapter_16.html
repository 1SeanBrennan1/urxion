Chapter 16: Buying Process – Aligning for Success

Blount shifts the focus to understanding the buyer's journey and aligning your sales process to their needs and expectations. He cautions against becoming a "buying process puppet" and emphasizes the importance of "shaping the buying process" to gain a competitive advantage. The chapter explores strategies for mapping the buyer's journey, identifying key decision points, and adapting your approach to create a more seamless customer experience.

Key Concept

Understanding the buyer's journey and aligning your sales process to their needs and expectations is crucial for increasing win probability.

Key Terms and Definitions

  • Buying Process: The series of steps a prospect goes through when making a purchase, from identifying a need to selecting a vendor and implementing a solution.

Main Concepts

  • Understanding the prospect's buying process is essential for aligning your sales process and increasing win probability.
  • The buying process can vary significantly depending on the complexity of the sale, the industry, and the individual organization's culture and decision-making style.
  • Average salespeople often fail to map the buying process, leading to getting out of sync with the prospect's expectations and a decrease in win probability.

Supporting Evidence

  • The example of the on-site childcare RFP process illustrates how failing to understand the prospect's buying process can lead to wasted resources and lost deals.
  • The danger of becoming a "buying process puppet," highlighting how salespeople can lose control of the sales process by blindly following the prospect's dictates.
  • The importance of "shaping the buying process" emphasizes how UHPs influence the buying process to align it with their sales process, giving them a competitive advantage.

Practical Application 1: Mapping the Buyer's Journey

Actionable Task 16.1: Research Your Target Market's Buying Process

Steps: Conduct research to understand the typical steps your target customers take when making a purchase decision in your industry. Consider their motivations, pain points, research methods, and decision criteria.

Resources: Industry reports, buyer persona templates, online forums, competitor analysis, customer interviews.

Obstacles/Solutions: Difficulty finding specific information? Focus on general buying stages (awareness, consideration, decision, implementation) and adapt them to your specific context.

Timeline: Complete your research within one week.

Actionable Task 16.2: Create a Buyer Journey Map

Steps: Visually map out the steps your ideal customer takes, including their thoughts, feelings, and actions at each stage. Identify potential touchpoints where your sales process can align and add value.

Resources: Whiteboard, sticky notes, buyer journey mapping templates.

Obstacles/Solutions: Feeling overwhelmed by complexity? Focus on the key stages and decision points, and keep your map simple and clear.

Timeline: Create your initial buyer journey map within two weeks. Review and refine it quarterly as you gain more insights.

Evaluation

Success is measured by your ability to articulate the buyer's journey in your own words, identify key decision points and influencers, and align your sales activities to the buyer's needs at each stage.

Critical Thinking Resources

Critical Thinking Questions

  • How can you effectively map the buying process for your prospects, and what questions should you ask to gather this information?
  • What are some common challenges in understanding and aligning with the buying process, and how can you overcome them?
  • How can you "shape" the buying process to increase win probability without being manipulative or disrespectful?

Supporting Points

  • Asking open-ended questions about the prospect's past buying experiences, their decision-making criteria, and their internal approval process can help you map their buying process.
  • Collaboration, flexibility, and a willingness to adapt your sales process to the prospect's needs can help you stay in sync and maintain momentum.
  • Offering valuable insights, addressing the prospect's concerns, and demonstrating a genuine understanding of their challenges can influence the buying process without being manipulative.

Counterpoints

  • Some might argue that attempting to shape the buying process is manipulative and disrespectful of the prospect's autonomy.
  • The buying process can be complex and fluid, making it difficult to map accurately and align with perfectly.
  • Focusing too much on the buying process might distract salespeople from the core aspects of selling, such as building relationships and presenting value.

Rebuttals

  • Shaping the buying process is about adding value and creating a smoother experience for the prospect, not about manipulating them into making a decision.
  • While the buying process might not be perfectly linear, understanding the key stages and decision points can significantly improve your chances of success.
  • Mapping the buying process should enhance your selling efforts by providing insights into the prospect's needs, priorities, and decision-making style, allowing you to tailor your approach accordingly.

Discussion Topics

  • The ethics of influencing the buying process.
  • The role of technology in shaping and managing the buying process.
  • The challenges of aligning sales and buying processes in complex sales environments.

Argument Builder

Supporting Points

  • Understanding the buying process allows salespeople to anticipate stakeholder needs, tailor their approach, and present solutions that align with the prospect's priorities.
  • By shaping the buying process, salespeople can create a more efficient and predictable path to closing, increasing win probability and reducing the risk of stalls.
  • Aligning the sales process with the buying process enhances the customer experience, leading to stronger relationships and increased customer satisfaction.

Counterpoints

  • Some might argue that focusing too much on the buying process can make the sales process feel overly structured and impersonal.
  • Stakeholders might perceive attempts to shape the buying process as a lack of respect for their established procedures.
  • Overemphasis on process can distract salespeople from the human element of selling and the importance of building genuine connections.

Rebuttals

  • The buying process should be viewed as a framework for collaboration, not as a rigid set of rules. Flexibility and adaptability are still crucial for tailoring your approach to individual customer needs.
  • Transparency and open communication can help address any concerns stakeholders might have about your efforts to shape the buying process.
  • Understanding and aligning with the buying process should enhance your ability to build relationships and deliver value by providing insights into the prospect's needs and priorities.
