The Ultimate Guide to Cold Calling for Startups

I. Introduction

Josh Braun's Approach

Start with a hook to grab attention, then introduce yourself and your company

  • Example: "Are you tired of [common problem]?"
  • Pros: grabs attention quickly, sets the tone for the call
  • Cons: may come across as too salesy, doesn't personalize the call

Cold to Committed's Approach

Start with a personalized statement, then introduce yourself and your company

  • Example: "I saw that [company] is [briefly mention something relevant]"
  • Pros: personalizes the call, shows you've done research
  • Cons: may take too long to get to the point, requires research

Sandler's Approach

Start with a question to pique interest, then introduce yourself and your company

  • Example: "What's the biggest challenge you're facing with [specific area]?"
  • Pros: encourages engagement, gets the prospect thinking
  • Cons: may not work if the question is too vague, requires active listening

II. Handling Initial Objections

Josh Braun's Approach

Acknowledge the objection, then pivot to a question or statement

  • Example: "I understand you're busy, but can I ask, are you happy with your current [solution]?"
  • Pros: addresses the objection directly, keeps the conversation moving
  • Cons: may come across as dismissive, doesn't fully address the concern

Cold to Committed's Approach

Empathize with the prospect, then offer a solution or alternative

  • Example: "I understand you're on a tight budget, but have you considered [alternative solution]?"
  • Pros: shows understanding, offers a solution
  • Cons: may take too long to get to the solution, requires active listening

Sandler's Approach

Use the Feel-Felt-Found framework to acknowledge and address the objection

  • Example: "I understand you feel [emotion], and many of our clients have felt the same way. They've found that [solution] has helped them [achieve desired outcome]."

    III. Presenting Value and Building Interest

    Josh Braun's Approach

    Focus on the benefits and results of your solution

    • Example: "Our solution has helped companies like yours increase [desirable metric] by [percentage]."
    • Pros: clearly communicates value, focuses on results
    • Cons: may come across as too focused on features, doesn't build rapport

    Cold to Committed's Approach

    Use storytelling to illustrate the value and impact of your solution

    • Example: "One of our clients was struggling with [common problem], but after implementing our solution, they were able to [achieve desired outcome]."
    • Pros: builds rapport, makes the solution more relatable
    • Cons: may take too long to get to the point, requires good storytelling skills

    Sandler's Approach

    Use the Pain-Pleasure-Priority framework to understand the prospect's motivations

    • Example: "Can you tell me more about what's causing you pain in your current [process/system]?"
    • Pros: helps understand the prospect's motivations, builds rapport
    • Cons: may take too long to get to the solution, requires active listening
