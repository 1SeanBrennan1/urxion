Chapter 6: Tailoring for Resonance

This chapter tackles the challenge of tailoring the teaching message to resonate with different stakeholders in a consensus buying environment. Decision makers prioritize widespread support, while influencers and end users value being taught something new and relevant to their roles. Effective tailoring requires understanding the specific customer outcomes that each stakeholder is trying to achieve. The authors recommend developing stakeholder profiles and "functional bias cards" to help reps personalize their message and build stronger relationships with each individual.

Key Concepts

  • Consensus Buying: Decision-making often involves multiple stakeholders.
  • Tailoring: Adapting your message to resonate with each stakeholder's specific priorities and context.
  • Customer Outcomes: Understanding what individual stakeholders are trying to achieve in their roles.
  • Practical Application: Create tools and training for tailoring.

Explanation

Equip reps to tailor their message to different stakeholders within the customer organization.

Actionable Tasks

1. Develop Stakeholder Profiles

Steps:

  1. Identify the common stakeholder roles involved in your typical sales process.
  2. Create detailed profiles for each role, outlining their responsibilities, goals, motivations, and likely concerns.

Resources: Sales team, customer interviews, industry research.

Obstacles & Solutions: Difficulty obtaining stakeholder information - leverage existing customer data, conduct surveys, or partner with sales reps who have strong customer relationships.

Timeline: 3 weeks.

2. Build Tailoring Tools (e.g., "Functional Bias Cards")

Steps:

  1. For each stakeholder profile, create a tool that maps their desired outcomes to your supplier capabilities.
  2. Provide guidance on the specific language and messaging that will resonate with each stakeholder.

Resources: Marketing team, sales enablement team, top-performing Challengers.

Obstacles & Solutions: Complexity of tailoring tools - keep them simple, visual, and easy for reps to use in real-time customer conversations.

Timeline: 4 weeks.

3. Train Reps on Tailoring Techniques

Steps:

  1. Incorporate tailoring into your Challenger Development Program.
  2. Use role-playing exercises and real-world scenarios to practice tailoring skills.
  3. Provide feedback on reps' ability to adapt their message to different stakeholders.

Resources: Training professionals, sales managers, tailoring tools.

Obstacles & Solutions: Reps defaulting to a "one-size-fits-all" approach - provide clear examples of successful tailoring and reinforce the importance of personalization through coaching.

Timeline: Ongoing as part of Challenger Development.

Evaluation Criteria

  • Improved rep understanding of stakeholder motivations and priorities.
  • Increased rep confidence in tailoring their message to different audiences.
  • Evidence of tailored messaging in customer interactions (review call recordings, presentations, etc.).
