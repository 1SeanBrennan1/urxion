Chapter 8: Sales Outsourcing

Recognizing the importance of efficiency and delegation, Chapter 8 explores the benefits of sales outsourcing. It guides readers through identifying tasks in their sales process that are best suited for outsourcing to virtual assistants (VAs), such as lead list building, lead research, data entry, and email outreach.

Key Terms and Definitions

  • Virtual Assistant (VA): A remote worker who provides administrative, technical, or creative assistance to clients.

Main Concepts

  • Outsourcing tasks to VAs can free up time for salespeople to focus on higher-value activities.
  • VAs can handle tasks like list building, lead research, data entry, email outreach, and more.

Supporting Evidence

  • oDesk and TaskUs are highlighted as platforms for finding and hiring VAs.
  • A sample job posting for a lead generation VA is provided.

Critical Thinking Questions

  • What specific tasks in your sales process would be best suited for outsourcing to a VA?
  • What are the potential challenges and benefits of managing a remote team of VAs?

Discussion Topics

  • The impact of outsourcing on the sales profession.
  • Ethical considerations of hiring and managing VAs, including fair wages and working conditions.

Implementation Plan

Practical Application 1: Identify Sales Tasks for Outsourcing

Actionable Task 1.1: List Your Current Sales Activities

  1. Break Down Your Sales Process: Create a detailed list of every activity you do as part of your sales process, from initial lead generation to closing deals.
  2. Categorize Activities: Group your activities into categories, such as:
    • Lead Generation: Finding and qualifying new leads.
    • Research: Gathering information on prospects and their companies.
    • Outreach: Making initial contact with leads through email, phone, or social media.
    • Data Entry: Inputting data into your CRM or other systems.
    • Scheduling: Coordinating meetings and calls with prospects.
    • Follow-Up: Staying in touch with leads and nurturing relationships.

Resources:

Your sales process documentation, brainstorming sessions.

Obstacles and Solutions:

  • Obstacle: Difficulty breaking down your process – Solution: Review the stages and checklists you created in Chapter 7, online reviews of outsourcing platforms.
  • Obstacle: Concerns about managing remote workers – Solution: Start with a smaller project to test the waters. Choose a platform that provides tools to help you communicate effectively and monitor progress.

Timeline:

1-2 days

Actionable Task 1.2: Create a Detailed Job Posting

  1. Define the Scope of Work: Clearly outline the specific tasks you want the VA to perform.
  2. Set Expectations: State your requirements for experience, skills, communication, availability, and work style.
  3. Include a Sample Task: To screen candidates, include a small sample task (e.g., finding 5 leads that meet specific criteria).

Example Job Posting (Lead Research VA):

Title: Virtual Assistant - Lead Research

Description: We're looking for a highly organized and detail-oriented virtual assistant to help us with lead research. You will be responsible for gathering information on prospects from their websites, social media profiles, and online publications. You should have strong internet research skills, be proficient in using spreadsheets, and have excellent English communication skills.

Sample Task: Please find 5 companies in the [industry] industry with over 100 employees that are located in [location]. Please provide the company name, website, and LinkedIn profile URL for each company.

Resources:

Job posting templates (available online), your activity list from Task 1.1.

Obstacles and Solutions:

  • Obstacle: Attracting qualified candidates – Solution: Offer a competitive hourly rate, write a clear and engaging job posting, and highlight the benefits of working for your company.

Timeline:

1-2 days

Actionable Task 1.3: Screen Candidates and Make Your Hire

  1. Review Applications: Carefully review applications and select candidates who meet your requirements.
  2. Conduct Interviews: Interview shortlisted candidates via video call to assess their skills, experience, and communication style.
  3. Check References: Verify references to confirm the candidate's past work experience.
  4. Make an Offer: Offer the job to your chosen candidate and agree on an hourly rate and working hours.

Resources:

Your chosen outsourcing platform's communication tools, video conferencing software.

Obstacles and Solutions:

  • Obstacle: Finding the right fit – Solution: Be clear about your expectations, ask specific questions during interviews, and trust your gut instinct.

Timeline:

1-2 weeks

Practical Application 2: Manage and Train Your VAs Effectively

Actionable Task 2.1: Create Clear Instructions and Training Materials

  1. Document Processes: Create step-by-step guides for the tasks you're outsourcing.
  2. Provide Examples: Include visual aids (screenshots, videos) and examples to illustrate the desired outcome.
  3. Use Project Management Tools: Utilize project management software (Asana, Trello, etc.) to assign tasks, track progress, and communicate with your VAs.

Resources:

Project management software, screen recording tools, document creation software.

Obstacles and Solutions:

  • Obstacle: Communication barriers – Solution: Use clear and concise language, provide regular feedback, and be patient with your VAs as they learn your processes.

Timeline:

Ongoing (create training materials as you outsource new tasks).

Actionable Task 2.2: Establish Communication Protocols and Feedback Loops

  1. Set Regular Check-Ins: Schedule weekly or bi-weekly meetings to discuss progress, address questions, and provide feedback.
  2. Use Communication Tools: Choose communication methods that work for both you and your VAs (email, instant messaging, video calls).
  3. Encourage Open Communication: Create an environment where your VAs feel comfortable asking questions and providing feedback.

Resources:

Video conferencing software, instant messaging tools, email.

Obstacles and Solutions:

  • Obstacle: Time zone differences – Solution: Be mindful of time zones and schedule meetings at times that work for everyone. Use asynchronous communication (email, project management tools) to avoid interrupting your VAs' workflow.

Timeline:

Ongoing

Evaluation Criteria

  • Task Completion: Are your VAs completing tasks accurately and efficiently?
  • Time Savings: Are you able to free up your time to focus on higher-value sales activities?
  • VA Satisfaction: Are your VAs engaged and motivated to do their best work?

Additional Resources

  • Books: "The 4-Hour Workweek" by Tim Ferriss (a classic on outsourcing and productivity), "Virtual Freedom" by Chris Ducker (a guide to building and managing a virtual team).
  • Online: Upwork Help Center (help.upwork.com), TaskUs Blog (www.taskus.com/blog).

Outsourcing can be a game-changer for sales productivity. By carefully selecting and managing your VAs, you can create a powerful team that supports your sales efforts and allows you to focus on what you do best. On to the next sales hack!
