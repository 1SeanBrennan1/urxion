Chapter 11: Self-Control – Mastering Your Emotions

This chapter focuses on the importance of managing disruptive emotions that can sabotage sales success. Blount explains the fight-or-flight response and how it triggers emotional reactivity, leading to poor decisions and damaged relationships. He provides practical strategies for developing self-control, including identifying emotional triggers, practicing mindfulness, using the "Push Pause" technique, and consciously choosing your behaviors.

Key Concept

Self-control is crucial for managing disruptive emotions, making rational decisions, and maintaining a professional demeanor, especially in challenging situations.

Key Terms and Definitions

  • Self-Control: The ability to manage and regulate your emotions and behaviors, especially in challenging or stressful situations.
  • Disruptive Emotions: Feelings that hijack your rational thinking, cloud your judgment, and lead to unproductive behaviors.
  • Fight-or-Flight Response: A physiological and psychological reaction to perceived threats, triggering a surge of adrenaline and a shift in focus toward survival.
  • Cognitive Biases: Systematic errors in thinking that arise from the way our brains process information.
  • Dual Process: The ability to balance the focus on building relationships and achieving sales objectives while managing disruptive emotions.

Main Concepts

  • Self-control is a critical component of Sales EQ and essential for navigating the emotional rollercoaster of sales.
  • Disruptive emotions, often triggered by the fight-or-flight response, can derail sales conversations and sabotage deals.
  • Developing self-control requires understanding your emotional triggers, practicing mindfulness, and utilizing strategies to regulate your emotional responses.

Supporting Evidence

  • The example of Shannon losing a deal due to her emotional outburst illustrates how a lack of self-control can damage relationships and sabotage sales opportunities.
  • The explanation of the fight-or-flight response highlights how our brains and bodies react to perceived threats, leading to disruptive emotions and behaviors.
  • The concept of "push pause" emphasizes the importance of taking a moment to gather your thoughts and regulate your emotions before reacting to a challenging situation.

Practical Application 1: Utilizing the "Push Pause" Technique

Actionable Task 11.1: Identify Your Emotional Triggers

  • Steps: Reflect on past situations where you felt overwhelmed by emotions. Identify the specific triggers that caused those reactions, such as certain types of prospects, objections, or pressure situations.
  • Resources: Journaling, self-reflection exercises, feedback from colleagues who have witnessed your reactions.
  • Obstacles/Solutions: Difficulty identifying triggers? Pay close attention to your physical and emotional responses during interactions and make note of what sets you off.
  • Timeline: Ongoing, as you become more self-aware of your emotional patterns.

Actionable Task 11.2: Practice the "Push Pause"

  • Steps: When you feel a strong emotional reaction, take a deep breath, count to five, and consciously choose a calm and collected response.
  • Resources: Mindfulness exercises, relaxation techniques, role-playing challenging scenarios with a trusted colleague.
  • Obstacles/Solutions: Feeling like your emotions are taking over? Excuse yourself from the situation briefly if possible to gather your composure.
  • Timeline: Practice the "push pause" technique in all your interactions, both professional and personal, to develop greater self-control.

Evaluation

Success is measured by your ability to remain calm and composed in challenging situations, reduce emotional reactivity, and choose responses that are aligned with your values and objectives.
