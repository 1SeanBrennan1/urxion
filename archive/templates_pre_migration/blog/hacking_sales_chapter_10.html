Chapter 10: Nurturing Leads and Sparking Engagement

This chapter explores the art of lead nurturing – building relationships, staying top-of-mind, and guiding prospects through the sales process. It emphasizes leveraging social media platforms like LinkedIn and Twitter to connect with leads, engage with their content, and use social actions as triggers for personalized follow-up communication.

Main Concepts

  • Lead nurturing involves staying top-of-mind with prospects, building relationships, and providing valuable content.
  • Use social media to engage with prospects, share relevant information, and identify trigger events for follow-up.

Supporting Evidence

  • This chapter discusses using social media actions (likes, retweets, shares, comments) as triggers for follow-up emails or calls.
  • Tools like Boomerang and Rebump are recommended for managing follow-ups.

Key Concept

Lead nurturing is the art of building relationships with your prospects, staying top-of-mind, and guiding them through the sales process, even if they aren't ready to buy right away. This chapter offers strategies to actively engage your leads and increase their interest in your product or service.

Implementation Plan

Practical Application 1: Use Social Media to Trigger Buyer Activity

Actionable Task 1.1: Connect with Your Leads on Social Media

Steps:

  • Prioritize LinkedIn and Twitter: Focus on professional platforms where your leads are likely to be active and receptive to business-related interactions.
  • Send Connection Requests: Connect with your leads on LinkedIn, personalizing your invitation message (e.g., mention a mutual connection, a recent trigger event, or a shared interest).
  • Follow on Twitter: Follow your leads on Twitter to stay up-to-date on their activities and interests.

Resources: LinkedIn, Twitter, your segmented lead lists.

Obstacles and Solutions:

  • Obstacle: Leads not accepting connection requests. Solution: Make sure your LinkedIn profile is professional and engaging. Join groups or engage in conversations related to your leads' industries to increase visibility.

Timeline: Ongoing (make connecting with leads a part of your regular prospecting routine).

Actionable Task 1.2: Engage Authentically with Your Leads' Content

Steps:

  • Monitor Their Activity: Pay attention to your leads' posts, articles, and shares on LinkedIn and Twitter.
  • Engage Meaningfully: Like, comment, or share their content in a way that adds value to the conversation.
  • Avoid Sales Pitches: Focus on building relationships and providing genuine insights, rather than trying to sell your product directly.

Examples:

  • LinkedIn: Comment on a lead's post about a recent industry event they attended.
  • Twitter: Retweet a lead's insightful article about a challenge relevant to your product.

Resources: LinkedIn, Twitter, social media monitoring tools (e.g., Buffer, Hootsuite, SproutSocial).

Obstacles and Solutions:

  • Obstacle: Finding time for social media engagement. Solution: Schedule dedicated time blocks for social media activity, use scheduling tools to automate posts, or delegate some tasks to a VA.

Critical Thinking Questions

  • How can you use social media to nurture leads without being too intrusive or "stalkery"?
  • What types of content are most valuable for nurturing leads in your industry?

Discussion Topics

  • The role of social selling in modern sales.
  • The ethical boundaries of using social media data for sales purposes.
