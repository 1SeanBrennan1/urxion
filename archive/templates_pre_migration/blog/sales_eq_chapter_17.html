Chapter 17: The Five Stakeholders You Meet in a Deal

This chapter introduces the "BASIC" stakeholder framework: Buyers, Amplifiers, Seekers, Influencers, and Coaches. Blount emphasizes the importance of mapping the stakeholder landscape, understanding their individual motivations, and developing a tailored engagement strategy for each key player. He highlights the power of building a coalition of support and leveraging the influence of amplifiers and coaches.

Key Terms and Definitions

  • BASIC Stakeholders: The five key roles that individuals typically play in a buying decision:
    • Buyers: Individuals with the authority to make a purchase decision.
    • Amplifiers: Individuals who champion your solution and advocate for your company within the organization.
    • Seekers: Individuals tasked with gathering information and evaluating options.
    • Influencers: Individuals whose opinions and recommendations carry weight in the decision-making process.
    • Coaches: Individuals who provide you with insider information, guidance, and support to help you navigate the buying process.

Main Concepts

  • Understanding the roles and influence of all stakeholders involved in a deal is essential for increasing win probability.
  • Mapping the stakeholder landscape allows you to identify key players, understand their motivations, and tailor your approach accordingly.
  • Average salespeople often focus only on identifying the "decision maker," while UHPs take a more comprehensive approach to understanding the entire stakeholder array.

Supporting Evidence

  • The example of the complex software deal with 22 stakeholders illustrates the importance of understanding stakeholder dynamics and aligning your message with individual needs.
  • The story of Tony securing a deal by getting there first demonstrates how early engagement with key stakeholders can shape the buying process and give you a competitive advantage.
  • The contrasting approaches of Penske and its competitor in a truck leasing deal highlight the importance of showing genuine interest, building relationships, and tailoring your solution to the specific needs of the stakeholders.

  • Negotiation and conflict resolution are essential skills for navigating complex deals with multiple stakeholders. By finding common ground and creating win-win solutions, you can address conflicting interests effectively.
  • While amplifiers and coaches can be valuable assets, building your own credibility and demonstrating your expertise are crucial for earning the trust of all stakeholders.

Decision Process

The often irrational, emotional, and nonlinear process through which each stakeholder in a deal makes commitments of time, emotion, and action.

Main Concepts

  • The decision process is where strategy, logic, and human emotions collide, making it a crucial area for UHPs to master.
  • Aligning the three processes of sales (sales process, buying process, and decision process) is critical for success.
  • Understanding the five questions stakeholders ask during the decision process ("Do I like you?", "Do you listen to me?", "Do you make me feel important?", "Do you get me and my problems?", "Do I trust and believe you?") allows salespeople to influence outcomes.
  • Social proof is a powerful tool for aligning decision-making among stakeholders.

Supporting Evidence

The chapter emphasizes that UHPs are masters at perceiving, controlling, managing, and influencing irrational human emotions. Average salespeople often mistake the buying process for the decision process, leading them to focus on organizational steps rather than the individual stakeholder's journey.

Practical Application 1: BASIC Stakeholder Mapping

Actionable Task 17.1: Build a Stakeholder Map for Each Opportunity

Steps:

  • For each deal in your pipeline, identify the individuals who play the roles of Buyers, Amplifiers, Seekers, Influencers, and Coaches (BASIC).
  • Determine their level of influence, their attitude towards your solution, and their key motivations.

Resources:

  • Stakeholder mapping templates
  • CRM software
  • Internal contacts
  • LinkedIn

Obstacles/Solutions:

  • Difficult to identify all stakeholders? Start with the most obvious ones and use your initial conversations to uncover other influencers.

Timeline:

  • Create your stakeholder map before your first formal meeting with the prospect, and update it as you gain more information.

Actionable Task 17.2: Develop a Tailored Engagement Strategy

Steps:

  • Create a personalized engagement plan for each key stakeholder, considering their communication style, motivations, and potential concerns.

Resources:

  • Stakeholder persona templates
  • Communication style assessments
  • Insights from your research

Obstacles/Solutions:

  • Stakeholders have conflicting interests? Focus on finding common ground, building consensus, and creating win-win solutions.

Timeline:

  • Develop your engagement strategy within one week of creating your stakeholder map.

Evaluation:

Success is measured by your ability to identify key stakeholders, understand their motivations, and build rapport with them. Look for signs of engagement, willingness to collaborate, and advocacy for your solution.
