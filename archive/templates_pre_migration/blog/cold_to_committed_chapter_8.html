Chapter 8: Everything Prospecting (From First Touch to Showing Up to the Meeting)

This chapter delves into the practical aspects of managing the prospecting process from start to finish. It covers:

  • Sequences: Creating and implementing multi-touch sequences for cold accounts, warm accounts, and rescheduled meetings, utilizing various communication channels.
  • Research: Practicing the 3x3 Research Method to quickly gather valuable information about your prospects for personalized outreach.
  • Multiple Contacts: Strategies for effectively working with multiple contacts within a single account, prioritizing the decision-maker and adding others strategically.

Actionable Task 1: Create and Implement a Multi-Touch Sequence

Create and implement a multi-touch sequence for cold accounts, warm accounts, and rescheduled meetings, incorporating various communication methods (phone, email, LinkedIn).

Value: A well-designed sequence maximizes touchpoints, increases your chances of connecting with the right person, and provides valuable information to nurture leads over time.

How:

  • Determine the optimal sequence length and frequency: Analyze your target audience's responsiveness and industry norms to decide the duration and spacing between touch points.
  • Vary your communication methods: Incorporate a combination of phone calls, personalized emails, and LinkedIn messages, tailoring the content to the specific touchpoint and prospect’s engagement history.
  • Include valuable content and resources: Share relevant blog posts, articles, case studies, or industry reports that address the prospect’s potential challenges or interests, adding value beyond a sales pitch.

Actionable Task 2: Practice the 3x3 Research Method

Practice the 3x3 Research Method, identifying three pieces of useful information about your contact within three minutes to personalize your outreach.

Value: Personalized outreach demonstrates thoughtfulness and increases the prospect's likelihood of engaging with your message. This method ensures efficient and effective research.

How:

  • Create a research workflow: Identify a few key websites and resources (LinkedIn, company website, industry publications) you will consistently use for research.
  • Set a timer for three minutes: Challenge yourself to find three valuable pieces of information within this timeframe, focusing on the prospect’s background, interests, and company challenges.
  • Document your findings: Briefly note the key information you've uncovered and incorporate it into your outreach message to demonstrate personalization and thoughtfulness.

Actionable Task 3: Strategically Work with Multiple Contacts

When working with multiple contacts within an account, prioritize adding them to your sequence strategically, starting with the decision-maker and adding others after three touches without response.

Value: This approach maximizes your chances of connecting with someone at the account without spending unnecessary time working on contacts who may not be the right person.

How:

  • Identify the primary decision maker: Research and confirm the individual who has the ultimate authority for decision making related to your product or service.
  • Add them to your sequence first: Focus your initial efforts on engaging with the decision-maker, using personalized outreach and a multi-touch approach.
  • Trigger the addition of other contacts: If you haven’t received a response from the decision-maker after three touches, add a secondary contact (stakeholder) to your sequence as a backup strategy.
