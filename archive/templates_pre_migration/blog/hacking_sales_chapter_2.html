Chapter 2: List Building – Part 1: Where Are Your Customers?

Key Terms and Definitions

Ideal Customer Profile (ICP): A detailed description of your perfect customer, including demographics, needs, and behaviors.

Main Concepts

  • Identify your ICP to target the right customers.
  • Determine where your ICP "lives" online (which platforms, websites, communities they use).
  • Analyze past closed deals to identify common traits among successful customers.

Supporting Evidence

Examples are given of how to analyze competitors' customers and identify potential low-hanging fruit.

Critical Thinking Questions

  • How can you leverage non-technical web scraping tools like Kimono Labs and Import.io to build targeted lists?
  • What are some creative ways to identify "low-hanging fruit" based on your product or service?

Discussion Topics

  • Ethical considerations of web scraping and data collection.
  • The importance of balancing quantity vs. quality in list building.

Key Concept

Target the right customers by defining your Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) and understanding where they spend their time online.

Implementation Plan

Practical Application 1: Create Your ICP Document

Actionable Task 1.1: Define Customer Characteristics

Steps:

  • Demographics:
    • Industry: Which industries are you targeting?
    • Company Size: What is the ideal revenue or employee count range for your customers?
    • Location: Where are your target customers located geographically?
  • Needs & Pain Points:
    • Challenges: What are the common problems or challenges your ideal customers face?
    • Needs: What specific needs do they have related to your product or service?
    • Motivations: Why are they seeking solutions in this area? What are their goals?
  • Behaviors:
    • Technology Use: What technologies or tools do they currently use?
    • Content Consumption: What websites, blogs, or publications do they read?
    • Social Media Presence: Where are they active on social media? What are they talking about?
  • Additional Factors:
    • Budget: What is their typical budget for solutions in your category?
    • Decision-Making Process: Who are the key decision-makers and influencers in the buying process?

Resources: Brainstorming session, customer data (if available), market research reports, competitor analysis.

Obstacles and Solutions:

  • Obstacle: Limited data – Solution: Start with assumptions based on your existing customers or industry knowledge, then refine your ICP as you gather more data.

Timeline: 1-2 days

Actionable Task 1.2: Identify Online Hangouts

Steps:

  • Websites: List industry-specific websites, publications, blogs, and forums that your target customers are likely to visit.
  • Social Media Platforms: Determine which platforms your ICP uses most frequently (LinkedIn, Twitter, Facebook, etc.).
  • Online Communities: Look for relevant LinkedIn groups, Facebook groups, Slack channels, or other online communities where your ICP gathers.
  • Events: Identify industry conferences, webinars, or online events that your ICP attends.

Resources: Your ICP document, online research, social listening tools (e.g., Brand24, Mention), industry event calendars.

Obstacles and Solutions:

  • Obstacle: Difficulty finding niche communities – Solution: Network with industry experts or use advanced search operators on Google (e.g., "site:[industry website] [keyword]").

Timeline: 2-3 days

Practical Application 2: Begin Building Your Initial List

Actionable Task 2.1: Start With Low-Hanging Fruit

Steps:

  • Competitor Analysis: Identify potential customers who are already engaging with your competitors (e.g., following them on social media, leaving reviews, attending their webinars).
  • Content Engagement: Look for people who are actively consuming content related to your product or service (e.g., commenting on blog posts, downloading ebooks, attending webinars).
  • Event Attendees: Gather lists of attendees from industry events, webinars, or conferences.

Resources: Your ICP document, competitor websites and social media profiles, industry event websites, tools like Kimono Labs and Import.io for basic web scraping (if needed).

Obstacles and Solutions:

  • Obstacle: Technical challenges with web scraping – Solution: Hire a freelancer with web scraping experience, use pre-built web scraping tools, or seek help from online forums.

Timeline: 1-2 weeks (ongoing)

Evaluation Criteria

  • ICP: Is your ICP document comprehensive and actionable? Does it accurately reflect your target market?
  • List Building: Are you consistently adding new leads to your list? Is the data accurate and up-to-date? Are you able to identify early wins ("low-hanging fruit")?
